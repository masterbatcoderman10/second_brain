🧵 My tips for getting the best results out of Claude Design! I’m on the verticals team at Anthropic which means I serve 7 different products. Claude Design makes it possible!
1. Set up your design system and your core screens. An hour of setup and refinement here is worth it

## 2. Ryan Mather (@Flomerboy)
- URL: https://x.com/Flomerboy/status/2045162323346690424
- Date: Fri Apr 17 15:27:49 +0000 2026
- Character count: 266

2. Iterate with your engineers live
I’m usually able to design new features with an engineer in a single meeting. Because Claude is super fast at mockups, we can stay high level in our conversation, riffing on concepts and constraints and watching them come to life.

## 3. Ryan Mather (@Flomerboy)
- URL: https://x.com/Flomerboy/status/2045162324688851018
- Date: Fri Apr 17 15:27:50 +0000 2026
- Character count: 222

3. Use the Comment tool to make rapid fire surgical edits
After a rough first draft, there might be dozens of details you want to tweak. It’s tricky to describe all the changes you want verbally - so don’t! Point and crit.

## 4. Ryan Mather (@Flomerboy)
- URL: https://x.com/Flomerboy/status/2045162325989106160
- Date: Fri Apr 17 15:27:50 +0000 2026
- Character count: 183

4. Ask Claude to make video demos of your ideas
In general, Claude Design can do almost anything you can think of. It’s honestly more like Claude Code than a canvas-based design tool.

## 5. Ryan Mather (@Flomerboy)
- URL: https://x.com/Flomerboy/status/2045162327356448937
- Date: Fri Apr 17 15:27:50 +0000 2026
- Character count: 279

5. Use connectors (esp. docs/slack)
Once set up, you can send prompts like “Please read the meeting notes from the product roast and create a deck exploring different design solutions for everything that came up”. Go for a walk and touch some grass and come back with fresh eyes!

## 6. Ryan Mather (@Flomerboy)
- URL: https://x.com/Flomerboy/status/2045162328593670321
- Date: Fri Apr 17 15:27:51 +0000 2026
- Character count: 277

6. Ask Claude to make bespoke on-the-fly tools
In general, don’t try to use Claude Design the way you would use a canvas-based tool. It’s a different animal with different superpowers. Experiment and get funky! You’ll find yourself designing circles around the way you used to.

## 7. Ryan Mather (@Flomerboy)
- URL: https://x.com/Flomerboy/status/2045162329801695338
- Date: Fri Apr 17 15:27:51 +0000 2026
- Character count: 261

7. Know when to slow down and do things by hand
New icons, spot illustrations, naming. Some details will always make an outsized impact. It can be easy to get sucked into the hyper speed of agentic designing. Knowing when to slow down is an art form of its own.

This eliminates the traditional designer/engineer handoff cycle (design → share file → engineer implements → back to design for revisions). Collapsing that cycle compresses feature development from weeks to hours.

This is the productivity upgrade that transforms design review from "write long Slack messages with screenshots and arrows" to "click directly on the element and describe the change." The feedback loop tightens dramatically.

This is the piece most coverage of yesterday's launch didn't highlight — the tool generates moving artifacts, not just screenshots. Interactive prototypes. The output isn't limited to "design files." It's "whatever computational artifact helps you think about the problem."

Tip 7: Know when to slow down and do things by hand. Icons, spot illustrations, naming. The human judgment calls that matter disproportionately. This is the professional humility that separates Claude Design from AI-replaces-designer discourse — the tool accelerates the 80% of design work that's execution, so humans can focus more attention on the 20% that's judgment.

The difference matters because bolt-on AI has fundamental limitations — it's trying to make a human-centric tool work with AI augmentation.
