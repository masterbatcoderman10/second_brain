# Seedance 2.0: The New Default Video Model

Seedance 2.0: The New Default Video Model

Seedance V2 is live and available in the major AI tool stacks.

The thing that makes it different is multi-input generation. Every video model before it let you feed a first frame and maybe a last frame. Seedance V2 takes up to two images, up to two videos, and an audio file, all in one generation, all tagged inside one prompt.

You are not generating a clip anymore. You are directing one.

Sirio, one of the sharper AI creative builders working right now, walked me through it on the podcast. This is what he showed, what he prompted, and how to use it to actually make money.

## Step 01 — Understand What Seedance V2 Actually Is

It is a video generator. It is also a video editor. Treat it as both.

Example Sirio ran live:

- Input 1: an AI-generated green screen video of two people in motion

- Input 2: character reference image one

- Input 3: character reference image two

- Input 4: new background image

- Prompt: replace the two characters, replace the background, preserve the motion exactly

Output in about 60 seconds. The motion control held. The characters held. The background held.

That job used to require a production studio, a shoot day, and a budget. Now it is one prompt and four inputs.

## Step 02 — Start With a Strong Source Reference

Sirio's rule, said twice in the conversation: everything starts with a very good source image.

You can describe your vision in words. The model will guess. Feed it a reference image that matches your taste and the model stops guessing.

His framing: treat the model like an assistant or a friend. If you told a human collaborator "make something cool," they would make a mess. Show them a picture of what good looks like, they make the thing.

This applies to every input you give it:

- Character reference: use a clean, high-quality image of the face you want preserved

- Background reference: use an image with the lighting and composition you want

- Motion reference: use a video where the motion is already correct

Good references in. Good video out.

## Step 03 — Write Longer, More Specific Prompts (Opposite of Kling)

This is the part most people will get wrong.

With Kling 3, shorter prompts win. Fewer tokens, tighter output. With Seedance V2, the rule flips. The more specific the prompt, the higher the fidelity of the output, especially for:

- Preserving character identity across the clip

- Preserving a specific motion from a reference video

- Preserving transitions between scenes

Sirio's workflow:

1. Write the first draft of the prompt himself

2. Paste it into Claude 4.6 Opus to optimize it

3. Run the optimized version through Seedance V2

His take on why Claude: it understands prompt engineering for vision models better than GPT in his testing. He flagged the bias. Use whichever LLM you prefer. The lesson is the loop, not the brand.

## Step 04 — Production Use Cases That Actually Make Money

Three that got demoed:

Virtual try-on. Sirio filmed himself outside in Montreal at minus 30 degrees. He then used Seedance V2 to swap his clothing with different outfits on top of his own footage. Brands can send zero physical product. Creators can publish unlimited variations.

AI influencer product review. Generate a character that does not exist. Place a real product in her hand with Nano Banana Pro. Animate her reaction with Seedance V2. She talks about a product she never tried. The brand shipped nothing. The content is endless. He ran the live demo on camera, including the "quick taste test" reaction.

Green screen replacement for games and landing pages. Replace characters, replace backgrounds, keep motion. One prompt replaces a full reshoot.

The pattern under all three: the brand is no longer paying for logistics. It is paying for prompts.

## Step 05 — Pick the Right Model for the Job

Seedance V2 is the new daily driver. It is not the only tool.

- Seedance V2: default for generation and editing. Multi-input. Strong lip sync. Strong character consistency. Best for logos, UX animation, ads, influencer content.

- Kling 3: cinematic feel. Stronger on emotion control. Better when you want film-quality look.

- Enhancer V4: fine-tuned for talking heads. Lower fidelity on purpose. Built for one specific use case and good at it.

- Google VO3: referenced for pricing context, around $3 per short clip historically.

Sirio's framing: creatives are not using these for fun. They are monetizing. So model choice comes down to three things — fidelity needed, speed, and cost per output.

Match the model to the job. Stop picking favorites.

## Step 06 — Where Adobe Fits (And Where It Doesn't)

A $106B company does not disappear because a new model shipped.

Sirio's read: Adobe stays relevant for creative professionals who need real control. 8K output. Frame-level edits. Post-production. The part of the workflow where the AI output is not quite right and a human has to finish it.

The analogy he used: photography. Digital photography took over. Polaroid still exists. Film still exists. Each serves a different need. AI video will not erase post-production any more than the iPhone erased Adobe.

His advice to Adobe, implied: build agentic post-production. The editor that edits for you. Do not try to be the generator. Be the finisher.

The practical takeaway for you: generate in Seedance V2. Finish in Adobe. Ship.

## What to Actually Do This Week

1. Open Seedance V2 in the AI tool of your choice

2. Pick one real job you have (ad, product demo, try-on, talking head)

3. Gather one character reference, one background reference, one motion reference

4. Draft the prompt yourself, then run it through Claude 4.6 Opus to tighten it

5. Generate. Note what held and what slipped

6. Finish the rough edges in Adobe

7. Ship the output to a paying use case the same day

## Final Thoughts

The interesting shift is not the model. It is the input shape.

Every previous video model asked: text or image, pick one. Seedance V2 asks: what have you got, and how should I combine it. That is a different job. That is a director's job.

Most people will use it to make slop faster. A smaller group will use it to replace production budgets for real brands at real margins. The difference between those two groups is not access to the tool. It is the quality of the source references, the specificity of the prompt, and the willingness to finish the work in a real editor.
