# WFH Extension — Evidence Pack for eData

*Compiled: April 6, 2026 — for internal use with Atif, Basma, and Ben*

---

## Current Situation Summary

The Iran–UAE conflict began February 28, 2026, following US–Israeli strikes on Iran. Iran has been hitting UAE with missiles and drones daily. The conflict is ongoing and unresolved.

---

## Key Facts to Use

### 1. Escalation Trajectory — Getting Worse

- **April 4, 2026**: Largest single-day barrage — **79 projectiles** (23 ballistic missiles + 56 drones)
- April 1: 40 | April 2: 45 | April 3: 69 | April 4: 79
- Pattern is **accelerating**, not declining
- *Source: UAE Ministry of Defence statements*

### 2. UAE Being Hit Hard — Not Speculative

- Since Feb 28: **507 ballistic missiles, 24 cruise missiles, 2,191 drones** intercepted
- **12 dead, 217 injured** (10 civilians from Pakistan, Nepal, Bangladesh, Palestine, India, Egyptian, Moroccan nationals)
- April 5: debris hit Dubai Marina building and Oracle building in Dubai Internet City
- April 4: Habshan gas plant hit — 1 killed, 4 injured, major fire
- *Source: The National, Gulf News, UAE MoD*

### 3. Nuclear Risk — Real and Documented

- Bushehr Nuclear Power Plant hit April 4 — **1 killed**, Russia evacuated 198 personnel
- WHO chief warned: strike could cause "regional catastrophe"
- Iran warned: radioactive fallout would hit GCC capitals, not just Iran
- IAEA confirmed no radiation leak but expressed deep concern
- Trump publicly threatened to strike Iran's power plants and bridges
- *Source: Business Standard, WHO, Iran Foreign Minister letter to UN*

### 4. US Travel Advisory — Level 3 "Reconsider Travel"

- US State Department: **Level 3 — Reconsider travel to UAE** due to armed conflict
- US Embassy ordered **non-emergency personnel to leave**
- All US visa services in UAE suspended
- FAA issued NOTAM advisory for commercial flights
- *Source: travel.state.gov, US Embassy Abu Dhabi*

### 5. Ceasefire Status — Nothing Agreed

- Trump set Tuesday (April 6) 8PM deadline with threat to strike Iran's infrastructure
- Iran previously rejected 48-hour ceasefire — communicated via **continuation of attacks on battlefield**, not diplomacy
- 45-day ceasefire framework being discussed through Pakistan/Egypt/Turkey mediators — **nothing final**
- Strait of Hormuz: 94% traffic drop, oil at $113/barrel
- *Source: Axios, The National, House of Saud, Al Jazeera*

## How to Frame This

**With Atif/Basma** — factual, not emotional:
- "The attack rate is accelerating, not slowing. April 4 was the highest at 79 projectiles."
- "The US embassy ordered non-emergency staff out — that's their government protecting their people."
- "My country's travel advisory is Level 3. If something happens to me there, eData has exposure too."

**With Ben** — if he pushes "others are working":
- "The company already extended WFH for employees who wanted it. This is consistent with that approach."
- "The travel allowance removal offsets any extra cost to the company."
- "I'm not asking for something for free — I'm giving something up to make this work."
