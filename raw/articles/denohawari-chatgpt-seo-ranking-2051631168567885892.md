# Roadmap To Ranking On ChatGPT In 100 Days

## Days 1–30: Solution Validation

the beauty of AI ranking is that it doesn’t rank those who have the most content

it ranks the brands with the clearest explanations

strategy, rather than volume

most people publish 50 articles before testing if any of it is worth reading

we’ll do the opposite.

step 1: extract pain points

go where your buyers are struggling.

search:

- sales calls - review last 10 calls. use fathom/fireflies to note down exact questions and objections

- reddit threads - search site:reddit[dot]com inurl:comments "your problem/topic"

- twitter - search competitors comments and mentions

- client feedback - support tickets, feature requests, churn surveys

make a giant list of everything you find

throw it into ChatGPT with this prompt:

“analyze these pain points and rank them by frequency. group similar themes.”

this way, you have a ranked list of exactly what people care about

step 2: write 10-15 “answer-first” pieces

you have just one goal here: clearly answer the following questions:

- “What is X?”

- “How to do X?”

- “Why does X fail?”

use this structure:

first 50 words: give direct answers to the questions above

next 100-200 words: why it works + how it works

rest of article: comparison charts, what not to do, FAQ

we used this exact structure to scale a client, Musicfy to 692K clicks and $1M+ in revenue in just a few months

step 3: distribute

- post summaries on Twitter/LI

- drop answers in Reddit threads. don’t spam links, just genuinely be helpful

- check for most site clicks, most shares on social media, most upvotes + replies

here are some metrics to watch:

- time on page

- session length

- site clicks

by day 30 you’ll have 3-5 clear winners.

now double down on these topics in the next phase

## Days 31–70: Programmatic Coverage

once you’ve found your winning angles, it’s time to create variations and scale

LLMs pull from patterns across many different sources.

if you only cover a subset of a topic, you look like a partial source rather than an authority

don’t spray and pray and hope something works,

systematically answer every question, variation, and possible use case

so no matter how someone asks something…you’re the answer.

one of our B2B SaaS clients grew 825% in 3 months after expanding from a few isolated pages into full topic coverage

step 1: deep dive angles

take the core ideas and angles you found in the previous step

break each one down further into:

- definitions

- comparison

- use cases

- mistakes

- advanced tactics

example:

“LLM SEO” becomes:

- what is LLM SEO

- LLM SEO vs traditional SEO

- LLM SEO for SaaS

- common LLM SEO mistakes

- case studies

“project management software” becomes

- what is project management software

- Asana vs Clickup vs Monday

- project management for remote teams

- common mistakes teams make

- case studies

step 2: build content clusters

don’t write standalone pieces

structure it like:

- 1 core page (pillar)

- multiple supporting pieces

each piece answers one specific angle.

step 3: consistent thinking

this is more of a tip rather than a step

keep your messaging consistent

- same definitions

- same frameworks

- same terminology

AI needs to be able to confidently associate you with that idea.

the goal of this phase is to own the surface area of the topic.

## Days 71–100: Entity Velocity

even if your content covers multiple angles, AI still asks:

“who should I trust?”

it looks for patterns like:

- is this person mentioned elsewhere?

- do multiple sources reference them?

- is their name tied to this topic repeatedly?

if you’re only visible on your own site, AI won’t pick you

many brands with strong websites often get completely ignored in AI answers simply because they weren’t mentioned anywhere else

your goal is to be mentioned across multiple sites and pages in connection with your topic

step 1:

get mentioned on other sites

- guest posts

- podcast appearances

- founder interviews

- case studies on other platforms

when someone searches you up, your messaging and name appear everywhere with a consistent message

step 2:

create “referenceable assets”

AI wants to find original wording and ideas.

- original frameworks

- named concepts

- data points

- contrarian takes

step 3:

keep messaging tight

if you talk about 10 different things, your brand image is diluted

you want:

- one core topic

- repeated everywhere
