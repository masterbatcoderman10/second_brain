I filtered $10,000/month AI business models from the garbage. Here's the FREE breakdown

Most people selling AI courses in 2026 are recycling the same dead list. Dropshipping with AI. Print-on-demand with AI. Content farms. All of it is gone or going.

I went through everything. Kept what works. Threw out the rest.

---

## THE $1M+ TIER. Hard to start. Impossible to copy once you're in.

Micro SaaS portfolio - don't build one big startup. Build 10 small AI apps solving narrow problems. One hits. That one pays $50,000-$250,000/month. Peter Levels figured this out years ago.

Service as Software - don't sell tool access. Sell finished results. An AI agent closing every support ticket. You charge per resolved issue. You're replacing a $60,000/year employee with a $200/month system.

Buy a boring business - laundromat, car wash, small resort. Automate 30-50% of operations. Labor costs drop. Profit doubles. The business is worth 3x what you paid.

AI cybersecurity - 24/7 protection from phishing and deepfakes. Corporate contracts. Almost zero competition because the technical bar filters everyone who learned AI from YouTube.

Venture AI studio and marketplaces - a factory for launching startups back to back. Or build the Airbnb for AI agents: a platform where agents hire other agents or humans to get work done. Long build. Massive ceiling.

---

## THE $10,000-$50,000/MONTH TIER. Real money. Real clients. Real problems.

AI automation agency - companies are drowning in repetitive work. You audit, wire up AI systems for email, leads, reports. They pay well because it cuts costs immediately. ROI visible in the first conversation.

Lead generation - AI agents parsing databases, sending personalized cold outreach at scale. Warm leads are the easiest product to sell. Every business needs them.

Voice and chat agents - the plumber missing calls at 11pm. The clinic losing patients to whoever picks up first. You build a bot that answers and books 24/7. Monthly subscription. Recurring revenue.

Corporate AI training - teaching teams how to actually use Claude in real workflows. Two days of training bills at $5,000-$10,000. Every hour you save their employees is money they can calculate on the spot.

AI ad agency - video campaigns for brands via Midjourney, Cling, Sora. Sells from $1,000 to $100,000. Production cost is a fraction of what traditional agencies charge.

---

## THE STARTER TIER. Under $10,000/month. Where you learn.

Digital products - prompt libraries, Notion templates, mini guides. AI writes the draft, you add expertise, sell on Gumroad. Passive income once live.

Faceless content - algorithms punish lazy AI slop, reward quality. Real storytelling, dynamic editing, TikTok Shop integration. Money from monetization plus affiliate products

Micro audience acquisition - buy small email lists or solo communities from individual creators. Monetize that base by plugging in your AI products. Low entry cost, immediate distribution.

Freelance AI services - SEO blogs, YouTube thumbnails, Shorts cuts, voiceovers. AI does the technical work, you add judgment. Clients pay for output, not hours.

---

## AVOID COMPLETELY.

AI trading bots - if they worked, they wouldn't be sold in a $97 course.

Cheap AI copywriting, basic data entry, generic subtitles - AI already killed the floor on all of it.

Print-on-demand - market is saturated with the same AI-generated prints on the same t-shirts. No way to stand out.

---

## THE LAUNCH FORMULA.

Never start with development. Most expensive mistake in this space.

Find the problem first -> pre-sell before building -> deliver manually using AI tools -> only then build the system. Consistent paying clients come before automation. Not after.

---

## BONUS. For everyone who read this far.

The most underrated AI business nobody talks about: mobile apps for offline cafes and restaurants.

Build one template app. Walk into local coffee shops and restaurants in person. Offer cheap setup plus monthly admin. Most have no app. Some have no website.

Three things that sell the owner in five minutes: push notifications for new menu items, built-in delivery orders, loyalty mechanics - everyone who downloads gets 10% off as a regular.

More downloads. More return visits. More revenue. You charge $300-500 setup and $100-200/month ongoing.

One city. Twenty cafes. $2,000-$4,000/month recurring from a template you build once.

The model that survives picks one direction and doesn't stop until something breaks open.
