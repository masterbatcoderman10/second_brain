The real definition of SEO (in 2026)

Over the last couple of years, several new acronyms have emerged to describe what many people are calling SEO 2.0:

- AI SEO
- GEO
- AEO

There are probably a few others, but at the end of the day, I define SEO as one thing:

**Search Everywhere Optimization.**

And if that's the definition, then there are a lot more components you need to pay attention to.

For example, if you're doing local SEO, you need to focus heavily on the local pack.

Why?

Because Google is still the dominant player in local search, and there's a lot of evidence, based data from Rankability, that strong performance in Google's local pack for commercial queries often correlates with strong visibility in Google's AI Mode, AI Overview, and Google Gemini.

These Google products are all showing similar ranking patterns.

But here's what most people don't understand about local SEO in 2026:

The local pack is becoming increasingly competitive because more people are realizing that it's one of the best ways to get real commercial traffic from Google.

Not educational content. Not "what is X" queries.

Commercial buyers. People with credit cards ready to spend.

And the barrier to entry is still relatively low compared to the payout.

While everyone is fighting over blog posts and "SEO 2.0" acronyms, the people winning in local SEO are quietly building real assets.

This isn't a new strategy.

But it is becoming more understood, more validated by data, and more competitive.

If you've been sleeping on local SEO — now is the time to pay attention.
