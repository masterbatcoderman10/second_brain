# How We Generate Millions Of Organic Clicks With Programmatic SEO

SEO is hard

you build one page, include a couple of keywords, and hope you can fight your way onto page 1 on Google

meanwhile, your competitors:

- have thousands of pages already indexed
- years of backlinks compounding
- entire teams focused on SEO

so even if you do everything "right"...

you're still playing catch-up

you publish more, wait for MONTHS, see small movement, repeat

and best case?

you gain a bit of traffic...

but never enough to drive meaningful revenue

SEO is a long play

but the thing is, it doesn't have to be

instead of trying to outwork companies 10x your size...

we changed the game entirely, and it all begins with one simple change:

**instead of thinking in terms of pages, think in terms of systems**

systems that let you:

- target hundreds of keywords at once
- capture demand that already exists
- scale output without scaling effort

search demand always follows patterns, and you can use this to your advantage

people search things like:

- "best [tool] for [use case]"
- "vs [y]"
- "[tool] for [audience]"

knowing this, you can map out these patterns and then build a structure that can be repeated across EVERY possible variation

...now you can capture the high-intent demand from each and every one of those variations, without spending thousands of hours building pages

we've used this system to drive:

- 692,000 clicks in 4 months
- $1M+ in revenue from search
- 800%+ traffic growth in under 90 days

and it all comes down to one thing:

**programmatic SEO**

with regular SEO, results are always tied to writing more content

with programmatic SEO, you can write a structure ONCE, then produce content at scale

and each programmatic page targets a specific demand at scale

here's the exact playbook we use:

---

# 1. "Best" Pages (Curation)

pattern: best [category], top [tools]

examples:

- best CRM for startups
- top AI tools for marketers
- best project management software

what it is:

rankings of tools/services for buyers ready to choose

why it works:

captures high-intent users at the decision stage

pro tip:

don't list everything, make a clear #1 recommendation

---

# 2. Comparisons

pattern: [x] vs [y], [x] alternatives

examples:

- webflow vs wordpress
- notion vs coda
- figma alternatives

what it is:

side-by-side breakdowns of competing tools

why it works:

targets users actively deciding between options

pro tip:

include "who this is for", that's what AI pulls

---

# 3. Use-Case Pages (Personas)

pattern: [product] for [audience]

examples:

- CRM for real estate
- payroll software for agencies
- task management for freelancers

what it is:

pages tailored to specific audiences or industries

why it works:

higher relevance = higher conversion

pro tip:

reuse the same structure, swap only the persona

---

# 4. Integrations

pattern: [tool] + [tool]

examples:

- hubspot + salesforce
- slack + asana
- zapier + airtable

what it is:

pages showing how tools work together

why it works:

captures users already using another product

pro tip:

this is the easiest way to steal competitor traffic

---

# 5. Templates / Generators

pattern: [type] template, [x] generator

examples:

- invoice template
- cold email generator
- resume builder

what it is:

ready-to-use assets or tools

why it works:

instant value → high engagement + sharing

pro tip:

make it usable immediately, no signup friction

---

# 6. Converters

pattern: [x] to [y]

examples:

- pdf to word
- USD to EUR
- kg to lbs

what it is:

tools that convert formats or values

why it works:

repeat usage + massive search volume

pro tip:

add related conversions to multiply traffic

---

# 7. Examples / Inspiration

pattern: [type] examples

examples:

- SaaS landing page examples
- cold email examples
- portfolio website examples

what it is:

collections of real-world inspiration

why it works:

highly shareable + strong discovery traffic

pro tip:

add filters/categories to increase time on page

---

# 8. Directories

pattern: [category] tools

examples:

- AI copywriting tools
- email marketing software
- CRM platforms

what it is:

structured lists of tools or companies

why it works:

captures broad research traffic at scale

pro tip:

keep updating, freshness = rankings

---

# 9. Glossary

pattern: what is [term]

examples:

- what is LLM SEO
- what is API
- what does CRM mean

what it is:

simple explanations of industry terms

why it works:

builds authority + internal linking

pro tip:

link these into higher-intent pages

---

# 10. Localization

pattern: translate everything

examples:

- qué es SEO
- was ist CRM
- logiciel CRM gratuit

what it is:

localized versions of existing pages

why it works:

new markets + lower competition

pro tip:

don't just translate, localize intent for that country/city

---
