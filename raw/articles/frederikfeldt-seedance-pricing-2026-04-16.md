You're criminally overpaying for Seedance 2.0 - here's how to get it 4x cheaper.

If you're generating AI video right now, you're probably using one of three platforms: Higgsfield, YouArt, or Fal AI and have probably paid them at least $100 in credits.

All three offer Seedance 2.0. All three charge you a massive markup for it.

## Here's what you're actually paying:

Higgsfield: $0.25 per second of video  YouArt: $0.37 per second of video  Fal AI: $0.25 per second of video

A single 10-second generation costs you $2.50-$3.70 depending on the platform.

A minute of finished video (which usually takes 4-6 generations with extensions and retakes) costs you $20-25 incl. re-generations.

If you're generating daily, that's $450-$660 a month. For a single model.

Now here's the part that should piss you off.

## What you're actually paying for

These platforms are not just charging you for Seedance 2.0. They're charging you for everything else they bolted on around it.

Higgsfield gives you access to 30+ models. Kling, Veo, Sora, Seedance, and a bunch of others you've never touched.   You're subsidizing a model zoo you don't use. Their UI is built for people who want to browse and experiment across every model on the market.   If you know what you want and you just want Seedance 2.0, you're paying for a buffet when you only came for one dish.

YouArt is the same story. Massive platform, tons of models, image generation, video generation, audio, the whole suite. Their pricing reflects the infrastructure needed to run all of it. You're not paying $0.37 per second for Seedance. You're paying $0.37 per second for Seedance plus the overhead of every other feature on the platform.

Fal AI is the most developer-friendly of the three and the closest to raw API pricing. But even they're adding margin on top of ByteDance's actual API cost. And their platform is built for engineers, not creators. If you're not comfortable with API keys, SDKs, and JSON payloads, fal is not built for you.

Every single one of these platforms is taking ByteDance's API, wrapping it in a UI, adding features most Seedance 2.0 users never touch, and charging you 2.5-4x what the actual generation costs.

## The real cost of Seedance 2.0

I got direct API access from ByteDance.

The raw API cost for Seedance 2.0 is roughly $0.10 per second of video.

Read that again.

$0.10 per second.

That 10-second generation that costs you $3.40-$3.70 on other platforms? Raw API cost is a single dollar.

That minute of finished video that runs you $20-$25 on other platforms? Raw API it's about $6.00.

That $450-$660 monthly bill? Drops to under $170.

You've been paying a 2.5-4x markup and probably didn't even know it.

## Why the markup exists

I'm not saying these platforms are scamming you. They built products, they have infrastructure costs, they employ teams, they need margin.

But the question is whether you actually need what they built around the API.

If you're a hobbyist who wants to browse 30 models and try a different one every day, Higgsfield is great for that.

If you want an all-in-one creative suite with image and video and audio in one place, YouArt makes sense.

If you're an engineer building Seedance into your own product, fal.ai is solid.

But if you're a content creator or AI video operator who uses Seedance 2.0 daily and knows exactly what you're doing, you're paying for a bunch of stuff you never open.

## Why you can't just use the API yourself

Some of you are reading this thinking "cool, I'll just sign up for the ByteDance API directly."

You probably can't.

BytePlus (ByteDance's international cloud platform) pulled Seedance 2.0 access for US-based users earlier this year after the Hollywood copyright backlash. The global API rollout got paused indefinitely. The official platform Jimeng requires a Chinese phone number and Chinese payment methods.

I have access because I operate through a Danish entity. Denmark doesn't have the same restrictions. I'm not getting around anything, I'm just in a jurisdiction where the API is actually available.

That's the gap I'm filling. You literally cannot get this pricing yourself unless you have a non-US entity set up in a jurisdiction where BytePlus is still serving the API. Most of you don't. I do.

That's it. No model zoo. No feature bloat. No subsidizing 30 models you've never clicked on.

Just Seedance 2.0, optimized for people who actually use it for production, at a price that's 2.5-3.7x cheaper than everywhere else.
