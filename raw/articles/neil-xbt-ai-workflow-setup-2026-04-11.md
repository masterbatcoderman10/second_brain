## The Weekend: What to Actually Learn

You have two days. Here is how to use them without wasting time.

Saturday morning: understand the landscape

The three tools that form the foundation of every AI workflow setup are Make.com, Zapier, and Claude or ChatGPT as the intelligence layer.

Make.com and Zapier are automation platforms. They connect applications. When something happens in one tool, they trigger an action in another. A new lead fills out a form, and the automation adds them to a spreadsheet, sends them a welcome email, and notifies the team in Slack. Without automation, a human does each of those steps manually, every single time.

Claude or ChatGPT is the intelligence layer. It is what transforms unstructured input, like a customer email, into structured output, like a categorized support ticket or a draft reply. The automation platform moves information between tools. The AI understands and processes the information along the way.

Spend Saturday morning watching the free introductory tutorials for Make.com on their official YouTube channel. The goal is not mastery. The goal is familiarity. You want to understand what a "scenario" is, what a "module" is, and how data flows from one step to the next. Three hours is enough to get oriented.

Saturday afternoon: build your first real workflow

Do not practice on fake data. Build something real for yourself.

Pick a repetitive task in your own life. Maybe you get emails and always need to log them somewhere. Maybe you want to pull social media mentions into a single document. Maybe you want to summarize long newsletters automatically.

Build that workflow. You will break it. You will fix it. You will break it again. That process of breaking and fixing is the actual learning. By the end of Saturday afternoon, you will have built something that works, and more importantly, you will understand why it works.

Sunday: learn the sales conversation

This is the part most people skip, and it is the reason they never charge $500 on Monday even when they have the skill.

The skill of building workflows is not what the client is buying. The client is buying time back. They are buying fewer errors. They are buying the ability to focus on the work that actually grows their business instead of the admin tasks that drain it.

Your job on Sunday is to learn how to translate technical capability into business value. Spend Sunday studying what kinds of tasks small businesses do manually that they should not be doing manually. The most common ones are email triage and response drafting, lead capture and CRM entry, appointment reminders and follow-ups, invoice generation and sending, social media scheduling, and internal reporting.

For each of these, practice answering the question: "What does this cost the business in time and money per month, and what does solving it actually look like?"

A business with two employees spending a combined three hours per week on manual data entry is spending roughly $300 per month in labor on a problem that a $500 automation solves permanently. That math is not hard to explain. And once a client sees it, the conversation changes entirely.

## Monday: How to Start the Conversation

This is where most advice falls short. People learn the skill and then wait for clients to appear.

That is not how this works.

By Monday morning, you have a concrete offer. The offer is a one-hour AI workflow audit, free, during which you identify the most time-consuming manual tasks in their business and show them exactly how automation would solve them.

If they want you to build the automation, the setup fee is $500.

That offer is specific, low-risk for the prospect, and high-value on delivery. You are not asking them to buy something they do not understand yet. You are asking them to have a conversation that will probably save them hours per week.

Now reach out to five people. Not strangers. People you already know who own or manage small businesses. Former colleagues. People you have met through your current job. Friends who run freelance practices. Local business owners, you are on first-name terms with.

Your message does not need to be formal. Something like this works:

"Hey, I have been learning AI workflow automation and I am doing a few free audits this week to practice. You would get a clear picture of which tasks in your business could be automated and what the impact would be. No pitch, no pressure. If I show you something useful, great. If not, you spent an hour and got a second opinion. Want to do a quick call?"

That message has a conversion rate you will not find in cold outreach. You are offering something free and useful to someone who already trusts you. Most people will say yes just out of curiosity.

Go into that call with three questions and let the conversation do the work. What tasks does your team do repeatedly that feel like they should be automatic? Where do things fall through the cracks because of manual handoffs? What would you do with five extra hours per week if you had them?

Listen more than you talk. By the end of the call, you will know exactly what to build. And if the pain is real, which it almost always is for any business running on manual processes, the $500 conversation feels natural because you are solving something specific they just told you they have.

## What $500 Actually Buys You

It is worth being precise about what a $500 setup looks like, because one of the reasons people undercharge is that they are not sure what they are delivering.

A $500 setup is typically one to three connected automations that solve a defined, recurring problem. It is not a complex multi-system integration. It is not ongoing management or support. It is the diagnosis of a specific pain and the construction of a solution that eliminates it.

A realistic $500 delivery might look like this:

- A lead form on the client's website triggers an automation that creates a record in their CRM,

- sends the lead a personalized email drafted by Claude based on what they submitted, and

- sends the business owner a Slack notification summarizing the issue.

The whole thing runs automatically from the moment someone fills out the form.

That workflow, for a business generating ten leads per week, eliminates roughly two hours of manual work. It also eliminates the risk of leads falling through the cracks because someone forgot to follow up.

Before you delivered this, the client had a problem. After you delivered it, the problem is gone. That is worth $500. Arguably it is worth more, and as you build experience and confidence, you will charge more.
