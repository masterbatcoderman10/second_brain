## WHAT THIS ACTUALLY REVEALS (AND WHAT IT DOESN'T)

Before going any further, it helps to know what's realistic with this system.

What you can extract:

- Every active ad a competitor is running across search, Shopping, display, and YouTube through Google's own transparency tools

- Their Shopping feed structure including titles, images, pricing, and which products they're prioritizing

- Their ad copy patterns including headlines, descriptions, CTAs, and the emotional triggers they're using

- Their landing page strategy including page structure, trust signals, offers, and conversion elements

- Implied keyword themes based on what triggers their ads when you search

- Whether they're running brand defense on their own name

- Signals about their retargeting and campaign types

What you cannot see:

- Exact bids or budgets

- Actual performance data like ROAS, CTR, or conversion rates

- Precise audience targeting

- Their complete keyword list (strong estimates, not the full picture)

The reality is that what you can see gives you about 80-90% of their visible strategy, which is more than enough to identify gaps, take their best angles, and build campaigns that directly counter their positioning.

## THE 30-MINUTE WORKFLOW

This runs in 5 steps and each one feeds into the next. By the end, Claude produces a full competitor playbook that your team can act on immediately.

STEP 1: PULL THEIR ADS FROM GOOGLE (2 MINUTES)

Go to adstransparency[.]google[.]com

This is Google's own free transparency tool. Search any competitor by brand name or domain and it shows every ad they've run recently across all Google placements.

What to grab:

- Screenshot or copy 8-12 of their ads, mixing search text ads and Shopping product listings

- Note which products show up most frequently in their Shopping ads because that tells you what they're investing the most behind

- Check the date ranges on individual ads because longer-running ads are likely their best performers

- Pay attention to the ad formats they're using since that signals which campaign types are driving their spend

STEP 2: SEARCH GOOGLE AS THEIR CUSTOMER (8 MINUTES)

Open an incognito window and run 5-8 searches that their ideal buyer would actually type.

The searches to run:

- Their brand name directly

- Their top product names

- Generic category terms like "buy [product type]"

- Comparison terms like "[their brand] vs [alternative]"

- Problem-aware terms like "best [product type] for [specific need]"

For each search, screenshot:

- The search ads appearing at the top

- The Shopping results showing product tiles with images, titles, and prices

- Whether they show up when you search their own brand name, which tells you if they're running brand defense

- What shows up in the AI Overview if one appears, because that reveals how Google's AI positions their brand relative to competitors

STEP 3: VISIT THEIR LANDING PAGES (3 MINUTES)

Click through on 2-3 of their top ads and screenshot the landing pages they send traffic to.

What to capture:

- The headline and the main offer

- Pricing and how they present it

- Trust signals like reviews, guarantees, certifications, and social proof

- CTA placement and language

- Page structure and the order information appears in

- Whether the landing page actually delivers on what the ad promised

These screenshots become the raw material that Claude uses to deconstruct their conversion strategy.

STEP 4: FEED EVERYTHING INTO CLAUDE (10 MINUTES)

Open a new Claude chat. Upload all the screenshots or paste the ad copy text.

Then run these prompts one after another.

Prompt 1 - Ad and Shopping Breakdown:

"You are a Google Ads strategist who works with $1M+/month ecom brands. Analyze these competitor ads and Shopping listings.

For each ad, extract the implied search keywords from the headlines and offers. Break down the copy structure including headlines, descriptions, CTAs, and emotional triggers. Analyze Shopping feed patterns including title format, number of attributes, pricing signals, and urgency elements.

Then give me their top 5-10 implied keyword themes, overall messaging patterns and positioning, strengths and weaknesses compared to ecom best practices, and what their product prioritization reveals about their strategy.

Format in clear sections with tables where it helps."

Prompt 2 - Landing Page Teardown:

"Analyze these competitor landing pages. Map the page structure from top to bottom. Identify their offer, pricing psychology, trust elements, urgency tactics, and conversion boosters. Tell me whether the landing page matches their ad promises. Then give me 3-5 things we could take or counter for our own brand."

Prompt 3 - Full Strategy Synthesis:

"Based on everything above, build a complete Google Ads competitor playbook for this brand. Cover keywords they're likely targeting with examples, their Shopping and product feed strategy, campaign types they appear to be running with evidence, retargeting signals, brand defense approach, landing page and offer strategy, and any gaps or weaknesses we can exploit. Format as an executive summary followed by a prioritized action list and 5 specific campaign ideas we can test against them."

Claude processes all of this and produces a competitive intelligence brief in under a minute that would take a strategist days to put together manually.

STEP 5: PULL OUT YOUR ACTION ITEMS (5-7 MINUTES)

Go through Claude's output and identify the moves that matter for your brand:

- Keywords they're ranking for that you haven't targeted yet

- Offer angles they're using that you could counter or improve on

- Landing page elements that are clearly working for them that your pages are missing

- Shopping feed optimizations they've made that you haven't, like stronger title structure, better images, or more complete attributes

- Gaps in their strategy that give you an opening, whether that's weak brand defense, generic Shopping images, or landing pages that don't match their ads

Take the top 3-5 action items and have your team start implementing them this week.

## HOW TO USE THIS AS AN ONGOING ADVANTAGE

Running this once is valuable but running it quarterly on your top 3-5 competitors turns it into a sustained edge.

The competitive landscape on Google Ads shifts constantly.

Competitors launch new products, test different offers, redesign their landing pages, and adjust their pricing.

The brands that monitor these shifts and respond quickly are the ones that consistently win more auctions at better prices.

Set a recurring reminder to run this every 90 days on your main competitors.

Each round takes about 30 minutes and gives your team fresh intelligence that directly shapes your next round of campaign decisions.

You can also run it whenever you notice a competitor suddenly appearing more aggressively in your Shopping row or bidding on your brand name.

Instead of guessing what changed, pull their full strategy in 30 minutes and respond with precision.

## BEING STRAIGHT ABOUT THE LIMITATIONS

This system works with publicly available data. Everything comes from tools Google themselves provide or from information visible in normal search results.

You will not get exact bids, budgets, or internal performance numbers. You're working with what's visible, and while that covers a lot, it's not the complete picture.

Ads and Shopping listings also change frequently, which means this is a snapshot of their strategy at this moment in time. That's exactly why running it regularly matters more than running it once.

Brands that rely heavily on Performance Max are also harder to break down because PMax blends multiple campaign types together.

You can still see the outputs, meaning the ads and Shopping listings, but the internal campaign architecture is less visible.
