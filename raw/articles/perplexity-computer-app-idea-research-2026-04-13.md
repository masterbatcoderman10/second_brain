# Perplexity Computer App Idea Research Prompt

**Full prompt used to find $500K/mo app markets via Perplexity Computer — replaces a week of manual research across X, TikTok, Sensor Tower, and App Store.**

## The Prompt

> I want to find my next mobile app to build. Run a full market research session. Go through every step below. Do not skip any.
>
> **Step 1: Creator Research**
> Search X (Twitter) for 5 app creators or indie builders who are publicly making $10K-100K/mo from simple mobile apps. Look for people who share their revenue screenshots, App Store analytics, or Sensor Tower data publicly.
> For each creator, find: X handle, apps built, claimed monthly revenue, tools used (Rork, Flutter, React Native, SwiftUI, etc.), distribution channel (TikTok, Instagram Reels, paid ads, organic), and whether their revenue claims check out against App Store ratings, review counts, and Sensor Tower data. Flag inflated or unverifiable claims.
>
> **Step 2: App Market Analysis**
> Based on what these creators are building, identify 8-10 mobile app categories generating real revenue right now.
> For each category, find top 3-5 existing apps on the App Store. Pull: average star rating, total ratings, estimated monthly revenue and downloads (Sensor Tower, AppFigures), last update date, and main 1-star/2-star complaints.
> Flag categories where top apps are rated below 4.0 stars, have not been updated in 6+ months, or have obvious feature gaps.
>
> **Step 3: TikTok Trend Validation**
> For each app category from Step 2, search TikTok for demo videos.
> Check: which video formats get the most views (screen recordings, reactions, UGC, before/after), specific view counts on top-performing app demo videos, whether the app concept can be demonstrated in 15 seconds, and whether couples/friends/reaction content could work as distribution.
> Only keep categories with clear TikTok traction or obvious viral content format.
>
> **Step 4: Scoring**
> Score every remaining app opportunity on 4 dimensions (each out of 5, total out of 20):
> - Virality (V): Can this go viral on TikTok? Is the demo visually compelling in under 15 seconds?
> - Build Difficulty (B): Can one developer build a working v1 in 1-2 weeks using AI coding tools?
> - Revenue Potential (R): Realistic monthly revenue ceiling based on existing apps in this category?
> - Competition Gap (C): How weak are current competitors? Is there a clear feature, design, or experience gap nobody is filling?
>
> Return ranked list sorted by total score. For each idea include: app category, specific gap found, strongest existing competitor and why beatable, estimated revenue potential with source, TikTok content format for driving downloads, and confidence level (high/medium/low).
>
> **Step 5: Deep Dive on Top 3**
> Take the top 3 scoring ideas and go one level deeper on each.
> For each identify: specific feature gap no current app fills, target user (age, gender, interest, geography), exact TikTok hook (describe video format), simplest possible v1 feature set (max 5 core features), and one reason this idea could fail.

## What You Get

- 5 real creators with verified/unverified revenue claims
- 8-10 app categories with revenue data, ratings, and competition quality
- TikTok validation per category with actual view counts
- Scored and ranked opportunity list
- Deep dive on top 3: target user, TikTok hook, v1 feature set

## Tips from Source

- Run this twice a month — app trends shift fast. Gap today, crowded in 60 days.
- Reject obvious ideas. If top result is something everyone is already building, market is saturated. Go deeper.
- Pair with App Store review mining. After picking an idea, run a second prompt to read all 1-star and 2-star reviews of the top competitor — gives your feature roadmap.
