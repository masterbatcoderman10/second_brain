## Step 1: Identify your hidden outcome.

Most founders pitch features.

"AI-powered." "Automated." "10x faster."

Nobody cares.

You'll get lost in a sea of slop.

Here's the only question that matters:

What does a users life look like after using your product?

Durable wasn't an AI website builder.

It was a way to replace your 9-5 income.

One positioning decision.

The difference between 100K views and millions.

[Embedded Tweet: https://x.com/i/status/2033957044714410368]

Here's how to find yours in 10 minutes:

→ Write down every feature your product has

→ After each one ask "so what?" out loud

→ Keep asking until you hit an emotion

→ That emotion is your positioning

→ Now write one sentence describing their life after using it

→ That sentence is your hook

We use Claude Code to run this automatically. The prompt:

```
"Here is a list of features for [product]. For each one, ask 'so what?' recursively until you reach an emotional outcome. Return the single strongest emotional positioning statement."
```

Feel the difference?

## Step 2: Engineer your video to score a 100

Every video we write gets scored on two things independently.

→ Invention Novelty: does this feel like something nobody has ever said?

→ Copy Intensity: does reading it make you feel something, not just understand something?

Everything in the video is scored and optimized.

We also run a dedicated Weapons Check agent in Claude Code before anything gets filmed.

Every single line in the script gets scored independently on both dimensions.

Both have to hit 10/10.

A novel idea with flat copy fails.

Sharp copy about a boring feature fails.

Lines that don't pass get rewritten automatically.

Lines that are pure filler get cut entirely.

The prompt we use:

```
"Score this line on two dimensions: Invention Novelty (does it make the product feel like a genuine breakthrough?) and Copy Intensity (does it make someone feel something, not just understand something?). Both on a 1-10 scale. If either is below 10, rewrite it and explain what was weak."
```

Here's how we rewrite weak hooks:

Weak: "Introducing our new AI platform"

Strong: "We built the world's first AI that makes your competitors obsolete overnight"

Weak: "Our tool helps you grow faster"

Strong: "The exact system that took 4 startups from zero to 1M+ views"

Weak: "Better customer support"

Strong: "Non-Mumbai 24/7 support that actually solves your problem"

The transformation from weak to strong is always the same.

Replace vague with visceral.

Features with feelings.

## Step 5: Create for the algos signals.

This is the part most people get completely wrong.

The X algorithm doesn't measure engagement.

It measures two completely separate things.

Signal 1: Sourcing.

Will your post be shown at all?

Retweets are the primary sourcing signal.

No retweets = post never enters the For You feed. Ever. This is why giveaways that require a retweet work.

Signal 2: Ranking.

How high will your post appear?

Replies are the primary ranking signal.

But not just any replies.

Reply chains where the original author responds back.

That single mechanic is the most powerful signal on the platform.

Every reply you post creates a new chain.

And every chain tells the algorithm this is a real conversation worth showing to others.

More chains = higher ranking.

Higher ranking = more people see it.

More people see it = more replies.

That's the cheat code to gamify millions of views.

You'll know whether your post is going to live or die in the first 3 hours.

Here's the exact reply schedule we give every client:

→ Post goes live

→ Let it breathe for 30-60 minutes with organic audience only

→ Reply to every single comment immediately

→ Deploy connections and influencers when there's organic velocity

→ Continue replying to every comment for 48 hours

→ Never stop. Not for meetings. Not for sleep. Not for anything.

We use Claude Code to help draft replies in real time during the 48-hour window so the founder never goes silent. The prompt:

```
I'm working on a launch [Insert video script, all of my knowledge base about them]. Write 25 potential replies to people who are replying to me about the launch to keep the conversation going, add value, feel like a real human responding to it, and keep each under 280 characters, preferably less than that.
```

Launches where founders follow this exactly

ALWAYS outperform by 2-3x.

## Step 6: Be strategic, not spammy.

Most companies make the mistake you should never do.

They blast every connection at once.

Or worse, they use influencers and tell them not to disclose they were being paid, by doing so break the law.

Awful.

We make it a mandatory requirement for each influencer to disclose.

If the algorithm sees a sudden artificial spike with no organic foundation.

It reads it as inauthentic.

The post dies within an hour.

Here's the secret key timing sequence:

Phase 1 - Minutes 0-60:

Let the post breathe.

Organic followers only.

Algorithm tests with your core audience first.

Reply to every question, thoughtfully.

Monitor engagement velocity.

Phase 2 - Hours 1-2:

Deploy 10-20 influencers if organic velocity is strong.

Stagger posts across 30-minute windows.

Phase 3 - Real time adjustment:

Watch engagement live.

Add gas when momentum is building.

Phase 4 - Next 24 hours:

Follow-up content while the algorithm is still hot.

Day 2: meme your product.

Day 3: deep dive on one specific feature.

Day 4: tell your company story.

Week 2: speak to the results results and show off testimonials.
