## The setup most people missed

Most creators pick one platform and bet everything on it.

TikTok creators hit 500K followers and earn $200/month from the Creator Fund. YouTube creators build a channel for 18 months before hitting the 1,000 subscriber threshold to monetize. Instagram creators grow an audience and have no systematic way to convert it.

Each platform has a hole. The system fills all three.

The framework: one 8-12 minute video produced per week. That video becomes:

> A YouTube upload (long-form, AdSense-optimized) Three 60-90 second TikTok clips (cut from the best moments) Three Instagram Reels (same clips, same captions, different hashtag strategy) One Instagram carousel (key framework from the video, reformatted as slides)

One production session. Seven pieces of content. Three platforms running simultaneously.

## 1 / 3 | THE NICHE: one niche, three platforms

The niche determines everything: what you earn per view on YouTube, how brands price deals on TikTok, what products sell on Instagram.

Pick the wrong niche and the math breaks at every step. Pick the right one and every platform pays.

The niche criteria:

Three filters. All three must pass.

Filter 1: YouTube RPM.RPM is what YouTube pays per 1,000 views after its cut. This is the foundation of the system because YouTube AdSense is passive income. It compounds as old videos keep getting found.

> Finance / investing: $10-25 RPM AI tools / software: $8-20 RPM Business / entrepreneurship: $8-18 RPM Health / fitness: $5-12 RPM True crime / psychology: $5-12 RPM Gaming / entertainment: $2-5 RPM

Finance content with 500K views/month earns $5,000-12,500 on AdSense alone. Gaming content with the same views earns $1,000-2,500. Same work. Different niche.

Filter 2: TikTok brand deal ceiling.TikTok's Creator Fund pays almost nothing ($0.02-0.06 per 1,000 views). The real money is brand deals. The ceiling depends on the niche:

> Finance / investing: $500-3,000 per post at 100K followers Business / AI tools: $300-2,000 per post at 100K followers Health / fitness: $200-1,500 per post at 100K followers Gaming: $100-500 per post at 100K followers

Filter 3: Instagram monetization ceiling.Instagram does not pay for views. It pays through brand deals and converts followers into buyers. The best Instagram niches for conversion:

> Psychology / relationships: digital products convert at 2-5% Finance: affiliate programs pay $50-200 per conversion Fitness: supplement brand deals + app affiliate at scale Business / AI: SaaS affiliate ($50-150/conversion recurring)

A niche that scores well on all three: personal finance, AI tools, business/entrepreneurship, fitness, psychology.

## 2 / 3 | THE SYSTEM: one video, seven pieces of content

The production system works in one direction: long-form first, short-form second.

A 10-minute YouTube video contains 6-8 strong moments that become TikTok and Reels clips. The key framework from the video becomes an Instagram carousel. One production session produces a week of content across three platforms.

Step 1: Research (Claude), 20-30 min

Every video starts with a validated topic. Not an idea. A topic with confirmed search demand, a specific angle competitors haven't covered, and a hook that works on both long-form and short-form.

→ Topic research:

```
I make faceless content in the [niche] niche on YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram.

Research and validate 10 video topics for this week:
1. Search demand (is this topic being searched on YouTube)
2. Competition (how saturated is this exact angle)
3. Hook potential (can the opening 3 seconds work on TikTok/Reels too)
4. Monetization relevance (does this topic attract the brands or affiliates I want)

For each topic:
- Title (YouTube SEO-optimized version)
- Short-form hook (under 10 words, stops a scroll)
- Why this angle is underserved
- What affiliate or brand category is relevant to this topic

Rank by: viral potential on short-form + search volume on YouTube combined.
Flag the 3 topics most likely to compound (get found months after posting).
```

---

Step 2: Script (Claude), 30-45 min

One full script. Written to work as a 10-12 minute YouTube video. Structured so that clips 1, 3, and 5 each work as standalone 60-90 second TikTok/Reels. The key framework from the middle section becomes the carousel.

→ Full script:

```
Write a faceless YouTube script for the [niche] niche.

Topic: [topic]
Hook: [chosen hook]
Tone: [educational / storytelling / data-driven / dry]
Target length: 10-12 minutes (approximately 1,500-1,800 words)

Structure:
- Hook (first 30 seconds — must work as a standalone TikTok clip)
- Problem setup (why this matters, specific data)
- Main content (3-5 numbered sections, each with a specific insight)
- Section 3 must be a standalone list/framework (this becomes the Instagram carousel)
- CTA at end (subscribe, comment, link in bio)

After the script, provide:
- [CLIP 1], [CLIP 2], [CLIP 3] markers on the 3 moments that work as short-form content
- [CAROUSEL] marker on the section that becomes an Instagram carousel
- YouTube title (SEO version, under 60 characters)
- YouTube description (first 150 characters — what shows before "read more")
- 5 tags (niche-specific, no generic ones)
```

Output as a numbered list, one slide per entry.
```

Step 3: Voiceover (ElevenLabs), 5-10 min

Paste the full YouTube script. Export the MP3. Then paste each short-form script separately and export three more MP3s.

One voice. Used on every platform. Consistency is the brand.

Avoid the default "Adam" voice. Every low-effort faceless channel uses it. Pick something in the tone of your niche: calm and measured for finance, curious and warm for psychology, direct for business.

```

Step 4: Video (editing), 60-90 min total

One edit produces four deliverables: the YouTube video + three short-form clips.

YouTube (DaVinci Resolve or Descript):

1. Import voiceover, sync stock footage to audio

2. Cut every silence over 0.4 seconds

3. Add lower-third text for key statistics

4. Background music at 8-12% (inaudible but present)

5. Export 1920x1080

Short-form clips (CapCut):

1. Import short-form voiceover

2. Auto-captions, position middle third of frame

3. Background music 15-20%

4. Export 1080x1920

The carousel is made in Canva. 7 slides. Brand colors. Same visual style used every week.

Step 5: Thumbnail (Canva), 10-15 min

The thumbnail is the most important frame on YouTube. It determines click-through rate, which determines how aggressively YouTube recommends the video.

Rule: one human emotion + one number + maximum 4 words.

No face in the thumbnail? Use a symbol, a graph, or a typographic design. Finance channels use stock charts. Psychology channels use bold single-word thumbnails. The "faceless thumbnail" is its own style. Audiences recognize it as a signal of information density.

→ Thumbnail brief:

```
I need a thumbnail for a faceless YouTube video.

Topic: [topic]
Key number or stat in the video: [number]
Niche: [niche]
Thumbnail style of top channels in this niche: [describe — data-heavy / dark background / bold text / etc.]

Write a thumbnail brief:
1. Background style (color, gradient, or image type)
2. Text (max 4 words — what goes on the thumbnail)
3. Visual element (graph, icon, symbol — no faces)
4. Color scheme (primary + accent)
5. What emotion it should trigger in 0.3 seconds

Also: describe a split-test version with a different angle.
```

## 3 / 3 | THE MONEY: three platforms, three income types

Each platform pays differently. Combined, they make the system more stable than any single platform alone. When TikTok is slow, YouTube compounds. When YouTube growth stalls, Instagram converts.

YOUTUBE: passive income

AdSense compounds. Old videos keep getting found. A video posted 18 months ago still earns every time YouTube serves it in search.

The RPM by niche (what you actually receive after YouTube's cut):

> Finance / investing: $10-25 AI tools / productivity: $8-20 Business / entrepreneurship: $8-18 Psychology / self-improvement: $5-12 Health / fitness: $5-12 True crime / documentary: $5-12 Gaming / entertainment: $2-5

Monthly AdSense math at a $15 RPM average:

> 100,000 views/month: $1,500 500,000 views/month: $7,500 1,000,000 views/month: $15,000 2,000,000 views/month: $30,000

RPM spikes in Q4 (October-December). Advertisers overspend before year-end. The same views that earn $15/1,000 in March earn $25-45/1,000 in November.

YouTube also pays for channel memberships ($4.99/month per member) and Super Thanks donations. Channels with engaged audiences see 0.1-0.5% of subscribers paying for memberships.

> 50,000 subscribers × 0.3% membership × $4.99 = $748/month additional

TIKTOK — brand deals, not the Creator Fund

The TikTok Creator Fund pays $0.02-0.06 per 1,000 views. At 1 million views, that is $20-60. It is not the income stream.

Brand deals are the income stream.

Brands pay based on niche, engagement rate, and follower count. At 100K followers in an engaged niche:

> Finance / fintech brands: $500-2,000 per post SaaS / AI tools: $300-1,500 per post Health / supplement brands: $200-1,000 per post Fitness / apparel: $150-800 per post

At 500K followers those numbers multiply 3-5x.

You do not wait for brands to reach out.

INSTAGRAM: conversion

Instagram's algorithm is built for discovery. TikTok and YouTube send people to Instagram when they want to buy something or go deeper. That is why the ManyChat automation matters: it converts passive followers into active leads.

The setup: every Reels video ends with "comment [keyword] and I'll send you [freebie]." ManyChat catches the comment and automatically sends a DM with the freebie link. That link goes to a landing page. The landing page has an email opt-in. The email sequence sells the digital product.

Affiliate income at scale:

> Finance niche: SoFi ($100-150/account opened), Robinhood ($5-20/signup + equity), Seeking Alpha ($50/subscription), NerdWallet ($30-80/lead) AI tools niche: Jasper (30% recurring), Copy.ai (45% first year), Monday.com ($40-150/conversion), Notion ($10-50/paid plan) Business niche: Kajabi ($100+ per referral), ClickFunnels ($38.80/month recurring), Teachable (30% recurring)

Monthly affiliate math at 100K followers with 1% conversion on 50K monthly link clicks:

> 500 conversions × $50 average payout = $25,000/month

That number is achievable in high-intent niches (finance, software) where the audience is actively looking for the products you recommend.

Digital products are the highest-margin exit.

A 90-page finance guide, a prompt pack for AI tools, a business templates bundle. Priced at $27-97. No inventory. No team. The ManyChat funnel delivers it automatically.

> 100K followers × 0.5% conversion × $47 product = $23,500
