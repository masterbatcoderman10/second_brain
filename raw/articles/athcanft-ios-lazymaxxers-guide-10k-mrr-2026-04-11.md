## STEP 1: STUDY WHAT'S ALREADY WORKING

There are tonnes of successful apps out there made by founders who've spent years failing time after time.

You can skip all that. Study what's working, understand WHY it works, then build your own version that improves on it.

Here are some apps printing money right now that you should be studying:

- Cal AI (food scanning, $3M+/mo)

- UMax (face rating/looksmaxxing)

- Pray Screen (faith/prayer lock)

Every single one of these sells to a core human desire. Every single one has a viral feature built in. Download them all. Screenshot their onboarding. Study how their ads look on TikTok.

The key word here is STUDY. Not steal. Not clone. You're looking for patterns. What emotion does the app target? What's the "aha moment"? How does the paywall sit in the flow? Then you build something that does it BETTER.

If you directly copy someone's app, two things happen:

- Apple rejects you.

- And even if they don't, the original founder reports you and you get pulled.

I've seen it happen dozens of times. Not worth it.

Take the blueprint. Improve the execution. Add your own angle. That's how every successful app founder on this platform started. They just won't admit it.

Creativity is the enemy of your first $10k. But so is a copyright strike. Find the middle ground.

---

## STEP 2: SELL TO A CORE HUMAN DESIRE

Every billion dollar industry is built on the same thing. Exploiting a core human desire:

- Beauty brands sell the fear of being ugly

- Insurance sells the fear of losing everything

- Dating apps sell the hope of not being alone

- Fitness brands sell the dream body you don't have yet

This is marketing 101. The only difference is now you can build an app in a weekend that does the exact same thing.

Your app MUST target a deep emotional trigger. Not a "nice to have" feature. A genuine insecurity or desire that keeps people up at night.

The deeper the insecurity the better. "Am I attractive?" will always convert better than "Am I productive?" Nobody is emotionally invested in their to-do list.

If your app doesn't trigger an insecurity, no amount of ad spend will save it.

The best app niches all tap into the same desires: attractiveness, health, wealth, sex, status, intelligence, identity, fear. These have been selling products for centuries. They'll sell your app too.

YOUR APP MUST HAVE A VIRAL FEATURE

This is the CHEAT CODE. If you can screen record someone using your app, or show the "aha moment" in a single visual, you have a winner. Face ratings, height predictors, food scanners all have a "reveal" moment.

VIRAL FEATURE + CORE HUMAN DESIRE = $$$

## STEP 3: ONBOARDING & PAYWALL

This is one of the MOST IMPORTANT parts of your funnel. And the best part? It's super f**king easy.

Study the onboarding flow and paywall structure from the top apps in your niche. You'll notice they all follow the same pattern. That's not a coincidence.

HARD paywall at the end. No trial. No freemium. Users must PAY to get their desire satisfied.

Pricing:

> $12.99/week + $59.99/year.

These are the numbers that work right now. Higher than you'd think. But the people clicking your ad are emotionally invested. They WANT the answer. They'll pay for it.

## Retention Offer

This is free money most people leave on the table. When a user tries to cancel their subscription, present a "retention offer". Set yours at $44.99/year.

Do NOT show a retention offer any other way, as Apple will reject it. This is the way I've done it for both of my apps and have never had an issue with Apple review with it.

The maths on this is insane. Every user you save from churning at $44.99/year is pure profit you would have otherwise lost.

## STEP 4: MAKE YOUR ADS

This is where most people overcomplicate things. You don't need a big budget. You don't need UGC creators. You need one format that works.

The Before/After Format

The highest converting ad format my apps is dead simple: before and after.

Show someone BEFORE using the app. Show the result AFTER. That's the entire ad. Do NOT "show off your app". People don't care about your app. They care about the RESULT.

For face rating apps, show someone getting their score. For fitness apps, show the body scan. For food apps, show the calorie breakdown. The "reveal" IS the ad.

Making The Before/After Format

1. Record yourself doing the "action"

2. Generate a guys face "before" and "after" versions with z-image ([https://fal.ai/models/fal-ai/z-image/base/lora/requests](https://t.co/NQrZw2v9w6))

3. Use [SwapTok](https://swaptok.app/) to make both videos (before & after)

4. Use TikTok Studio (free) to create the ad - add your screenshots, and add trending music

You can produce 10-12 variants in an hour this way. No creators. No contracts. No back and forth. Just you, your laptop, and an afternoon.

> DISCLAIMER: [SwapTok](https://swaptok.app/) is my product, I purpose-built SwapTok in order to make this process as easy as possible. You are more than welcome to use any other tool online.

Ad Rules

- Keep ads rough and native-looking. Trending sounds. Hook in the first 2 seconds. Show the viral feature. Trigger the insecurity.

- Your ad MUST look like a TikTok, not an ad. The moment someone thinks "this is an ad," you've lost them.

- Comment bait everything. TikTok's algorithm pushes videos with high engagement. Hide something in your ad that makes people comment (like men kissing). Comments = engagement = cheaper reach. It doesn't matter what they're commenting about.

- Check your comments and delete negative ones like "it costs money." These comments could potentially harm your ROI.

Start with a Smart+ campaign on TikTok. Optimise for subscribe/purchase events, NOT installs. $50/day starting budget. USA only.

Run 2-3 days before concluding results. You're buying data and investing in your winning ad.

You only need ONE winning creative. One. That's the whole game. Find it and throw money at it.

## STEP 6: MANAGE YOUR ADS

After 2-3 days you'll know if you have a winner. Here are the only numbers that matter:

- CPI < $2.00

- CTR > 1.00%

- CPA < LTV

When you hit those: SCALE. Increase budget by +30% every 3 days maximum. Don't increase too quickly or the TikTok pixel will s**t itself.

Make variants of the winning ad (10-12 vids). Same hook, different person / "story."

If you don't hit those: kill the ad. Make a new creative. Test again. Don't touch campaign settings. The creative is almost always the problem.

With the pricing model I mentioned, your LTV will likely land around ~$30+ per paying user. If CPA is under that, you're printing.

A winning ad can carry your business for MONTHS. LAZYMAXXING to its core.
