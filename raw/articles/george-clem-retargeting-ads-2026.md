# How to Print With Retargeting Ads in 2026

**Author:** George Clements | Paid House (@georgeclem)
**Published:** Sat May 02 2026
**Tweet ID:** 2050382424358445353

There's a gap in how most agency owners think about paid ads, and it's costing them a significant amount of money every single month without them realising it...

They run cold traffic campaigns to acquire new leads, they post organic content on Instagram and Facebook to build an audience, and they treat those 2 activities as completely separate systems.

The cold traffic campaigns generate calls. The organic content builds trust and grows the following. And the audiences those 2 systems create, the people who watched the content, engaged with the posts, clicked on the ads, follow the page, just sit there doing nothing.

But retargeting ads are what connect those 2 systems...

And the agency owners who have this set up correctly are generating conversions from warm audiences they've already paid to build, at a fraction of the cost of acquiring cold traffic, with dramatically higher conversion rates because the audience already has some degree of familiarity with the brand before they ever see the retargeting ad.

The ones who don't have retargeting set up are essentially leaving a warm, pre-qualified audience completely unmonetised while continuing to spend their entire ad budget chasing cold traffic.

That's an expensive way to run a marketing system, and in 2026, there's no good reason to be doing it.

---

## Why Retargeting Works So Differently From Cold Traffic

To understand why retargeting produces the conversion rates it does, you need to understand the trust gap that exists between cold and warm audiences.

When someone sees your cold traffic ad, they have zero prior exposure to you. You're interrupting their scroll, you're an unknown entity, and every claim you make in the ad is evaluated with a high degree of scepticism because they have no reference point for whether you're credible. Getting that person from "I've never heard of this" to "I'm booking a call" in a single ad impression is a significant ask, and the reason cold traffic CACs are typically $1,000 to $2,500 for a B2B agency offer is that it often takes multiple touchpoints before a cold audience member converts.

Warm audiences are in a completely different psychological position. Someone who has watched 60 seconds of your organic content, engaged with one of your posts, or clicked on one of your cold traffic ads has already made a micro-commitment. They chose to pay attention to you at least once. They've seen your face, heard your positioning, and formed some impression of what you do and whether you seem credible. The trust gap that cold traffic has to close over multiple touchpoints is already partially closed before they see your first retargeting ad.

The practical result of this is that retargeting campaigns convert at meaningfully higher rates than cold traffic campaigns targeting the same offer, and because the audience is smaller and more targeted, the CPMs are often lower as well. You're spending less to reach people who are significantly more likely to convert, which is about as favourable a combination as paid advertising gets.

## The 3 Custom Audiences That Form the Core of the System

The retargeting setup George runs at Paid House is built around 3 specific custom audiences, and each one captures a different segment of the warm audience pool that organic content and paid ads generate over time.

### Instagram Engagers (180-Day Window)

The first and typically largest audience is everyone who has engaged with your Instagram profile in the last 180 days. Meta defines engagement broadly here, capturing profile visits, post likes, comments, shares, saves, story interactions, and direct messages, which means this audience covers the full range of people who have had any meaningful interaction with your content rather than just the subset who liked a specific post.

The 180-day window is the right choice for most agencies because it balances recency with volume. A 30-day window keeps the audience very warm but often produces audiences too small to generate meaningful impression volume at a reasonable budget.

A 365-day window captures a larger audience but includes people whose engagement was so distant in time that their familiarity with the brand has faded significantly. 180 days hits the middle ground where the audience is large enough to run against consistently while still being warm enough to convert at rates meaningfully above cold traffic.

For agencies posting content on Instagram consistently, this audience grows over time as organic content accumulates engagements, which means the retargeting campaign is constantly being fed new warm prospects from the organic content system without requiring any additional ad spend on the acquisition side.

### Instagram Followers

The Instagram followers audience captures everyone who currently follows the account, and it operates differently from the engager audience in an important way: followers have made a deliberate, active decision to see more of your content on an ongoing basis.

Following an account is a stronger signal of intent than a single engagement event. A like or a profile visit could be momentary curiosity. Pressing follow means the person wanted to stay in the loop, which places them in a segment of your warm audience that is consistently more receptive to direct offer messaging than general engagers.

For agency owners, followers tend to be other people in the industry, potential clients who are evaluating whether to work with you over a longer time horizon, or people who've been watching content for weeks or months and are gradually moving toward a buying decision. Retargeting this audience with a direct offer, specifically a clear presentation of your service and a call to book a call, catches a portion of that group at exactly the right moment when they're warm enough to act.

### Facebook Page Engagers

The third audience is everyone who has engaged with the Facebook page you're running ads from, and this one captures a segment of warm prospects that the Instagram audiences miss entirely.

When someone watches your cold traffic ad on Facebook, clicks on it, comments on it, or even just interacts with the post in any way, Meta's algorithm records that engagement against your Facebook page. The Facebook page engager audience collects all of those interactions, which means anyone who touched one of your cold traffic ads but didn't convert on that first exposure is automatically added to the retargeting pool.

This is particularly valuable because cold traffic audiences who clicked an ad but didn't book a call are arguably the warmest non-converting prospects in your entire funnel. They saw the offer, showed enough interest to take an action, and then left without converting, which in B2B sales is often just a matter of timing or insufficient follow-up rather than a definitive "no." Hitting those people with a retargeting ad 24 to 72 hours later, when the initial exposure is still recent, closes a meaningful percentage of them without any additional cold traffic spend.

## How the 3 Audiences Work Together

The reason to build all 3 of these audiences rather than just 1 or 2 is that they each capture a distinct segment of the warm prospect pool with minimal overlap, and together they create a retargeting system that covers the full surface area of the warm audience your organic content and paid ads are generating.

The Instagram engagers audience captures the broadest pool of warm contacts from organic content. The followers audience captures the highest-intent segment of that pool. The Facebook page engagers audience captures the warm prospects generated by cold traffic activity who didn't convert on first exposure.

Running retargeting campaigns against all 3 simultaneously means that anyone who has meaningfully interacted with your brand across any of these surfaces is seeing consistent follow-up messaging, which is what produces the effect George describes as getting "absolutely slammed by retargeting ads" after watching organic content. That sustained, multi-day exposure to retargeting creative after an initial organic touchpoint is what drives the conversion rates that make this system work, because it replicates the multi-touchpoint nurture sequence that warm traffic naturally provides in a way that's systematic and scalable.

## The Creative and Offer Requirements for Retargeting to Convert

Building the audiences is the structural work. The creative and offer that run against those audiences are what determine whether the retargeting campaign actually prints or just burns budget against a warm audience without converting them.

The creative for retargeting operates under different constraints than cold traffic creative because the audience is already aware of who you are and what you do at a general level. Cold traffic creative has to do a significant amount of trust-building and positioning work before it can make an offer, because the viewer has zero prior context. Retargeting creative can be more direct, more offer-focused, and more specific about what the next step is, because the viewer is already past the awareness stage.

For B2B agency retargeting specifically, the creative formats that tend to perform best are talking head ads where you're addressing the audience directly and referencing the content they've been watching, social proof-forward ads that lead with a specific client result rather than a positioning statement, and direct offer ads that present the service, the deliverable, and the CTA with minimal warm-up because the audience doesn't need to be educated on who you are.

The offer itself needs to be consistent with the positioning your organic content has established. If your content is about scaling agencies with paid ads, the retargeting offer should be the paid ads service or coaching program, framed in the same language and around the same outcomes your content has been building toward.

Misalignment between the organic content positioning and the retargeting offer creates a disconnect that warm audiences notice even if they can't articulate it, and it produces lower conversion rates than a retargeting campaign where the offer feels like a natural next step from the content they've been consuming.

The primary text of the retargeting ad should handle the specific objections that warm prospects at this stage of familiarity typically have. These are not the same objections as cold traffic, where the primary barriers are "I don't know who this is" and "I don't understand what they do."

Warm prospects already know who you are and broadly understand the offer. Their objections at the retargeting stage tend to be around fit ("is this right for my specific situation"), credibility at a deeper level ("have they done this for someone like me specifically"), and timing ("is right now the right time to act").

Retargeting creative that addresses those 3 objections directly, with specific case study evidence and a clear reason to act now, is what converts warm audiences at the rates that make this system worth building.

## The Budget Allocation

One of the practical questions that comes up when setting this up is how much budget to allocate to retargeting relative to cold traffic.

The right answer depends on the size of your warm audiences, because retargeting campaigns have a natural ceiling determined by how many people are in the custom audiences you're targeting. A retargeting campaign running against 500 total warm contacts across all 3 audiences can only spend so much before it's showing the same ads to the same people too frequently, which drives up frequency, drives down engagement, and eventually burns the audience.

The general approach that works well is to start with a modest daily budget for retargeting, around $20 to $50 per day depending on your total warm audience size, and monitor frequency closely in the first few weeks. If your frequency is below 2 or 3 per week per person in the audience, you have room to increase budget. If frequency is climbing above that, you either need to refresh creative or pull back budget until the audiences grow.

As your organic content builds more engagement and your cold traffic campaigns generate more Facebook page interactions, the warm audiences grow and the budget ceiling rises naturally. For agencies posting content consistently and spending meaningfully on cold traffic, the retargeting audiences can grow to a size where $100 to $200 per day in retargeting spend is sustainable and productive.

The other budget consideration is that retargeting typically generates a much more favourable CAC than cold traffic, which means it deserves a higher share of budget than most agency owners currently give it. If your cold traffic CAC is $2,000 and your retargeting CAC is $500, the retargeting budget is generating 4x the clients per dollar of spend, and the allocation should reflect that efficiency difference rather than being treated as a small supplementary spend on top of the cold traffic budget.

## The Technical Steps

The setup process for this retargeting system in Meta Ads Manager (meta.com) is straightforward and can be completed in a single working session.

**Step 1:** Create the custom audiences. Go to Audiences in Meta Ads Manager, select Create Audience, and choose Custom Audience. For the Instagram engager audience, select Instagram account as the source, choose everyone who engaged with your professional account, and set the time window to 180 days.

For the followers audience, select Instagram account again and choose people who follow your account.

For the Facebook page engager audience, select Facebook page as the source and choose everyone who engaged with your page, with the same 180-day window.

**Step 2:** Build the retargeting campaign. Create a new campaign in Meta Ads Manager with a conversions or leads objective (depending on whether you're optimising for booked calls or form fills). At the ad set level, select your custom audiences in the audience targeting section, and exclude your existing client list to avoid spending retargeting budget on people who have already converted.

**Step 3:** Build the creative. Produce at least 2 to 3 retargeting-specific ad creatives that are distinct from your cold traffic creative in tone and directness. These should be shorter and more direct than cold traffic ads because the audience doesn't need the full positioning and trust-building sequence.

**Step 4:** Set the budget and monitor frequency. Start conservatively, check frequency after the first week, and adjust budget based on whether the audience can absorb more spend without frequency becoming too high.

The entire setup takes a few hours the first time through, and once it's running, the retargeting system operates largely autonomously, consuming warm audiences that your organic content and cold traffic campaigns are continuously generating and converting a portion of them into booked calls without requiring any additional hands-on management beyond periodic creative refreshes.

## Why This Specifically Compounds Over Time

The reason retargeting becomes more valuable over time rather than less is that the warm audience pools it runs against grow continuously as long as you're posting organic content and running cold traffic campaigns.

Every piece of organic content that generates engagement adds new people to the Instagram engager audience. Every cold traffic ad that generates a click, a watch, or a comment adds new people to the Facebook page engager audience. Every new follower adds to the followers audience.

The retargeting system is constantly being fed new warm prospects without any direct investment beyond the cost of the retargeting campaign itself, which means its efficiency as a proportion of total marketing spend tends to improve over time as the warm audience base grows.

For an agency that has been posting organic content for 6 to 12 months and running cold traffic ads consistently, the warm audiences can be substantial, often in the thousands across all 3 custom audiences combined, and retargeting those audiences systematically at a reasonable frequency is one of the most capital-efficient acquisition activities available.

The agency owners who set this up early, while the warm audiences are still small, build the infrastructure and the creative iteration history that makes the system significantly more productive by the time the audiences have grown to a size where the campaign can really scale. The ones who wait until they have a larger audience before setting it up are delaying the compounding effect that comes from running retargeting campaigns against a continuously growing warm audience base.

If you're posting organic content on Instagram or Facebook, or running any paid ads on those platforms, and you don't have retargeting campaigns running against the 3 audiences described above, the setup is one of the highest ROI activities you can do this week.
