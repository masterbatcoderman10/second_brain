## Why You Need to Target the USA (And How to Set It Up)

If you're sitting anywhere outside the United States, the first thing you need to understand is that TikTok's highest-paying audience is American.

The offers on Glitchy, gift card sweeps, lead gen, CPI offers are built for US traffic. If you're sending French or German viewers to a Walmart gift card offer, your conversion rate is going to be zero because those users can't convert.

This means you need your TikTok account to think you're in the US.

Here's how to do it properly:

→Get a secondary phone → an iPhone 7 or later works fine.

→Before you download TikTok, install NordVPN and connect to a US server. →Turn off Bluetooth and Find My iPhone.

→Set the region to United States in your phone settings.

→Then download TikTok and create your account.

From the algorithm's perspective, you're an American user, and it will serve your content to American audiences.

Don't skip this. It's the difference between hitting the right traffic and wasting every post on an audience that can't convert.

## Warming Up the Account Before You Post Anything

A fresh account that immediately starts posting promotional content will get shadowbanned before it ever reaches anyone.

TikTok's system flags accounts that show no organic behaviour and go straight into posting offers.

Warm up for at least 24 to 48 hours first. Watch videos in your niche. Leave a few comments. Follow some accounts. Let the algorithm build a profile of you as a normal user. Only after that warmup period should you start posting your actual content.

If you're running multiple accounts, which you should be, create no more than ten per device per day. For each additional account on the same device:

→delete TikTok,

→change your IP address through the VPN,

→reset your Location and Privacy settings, then reinstall and register.

→Let every new account sit for 24 hours before it touches a single post.

## Building a Landing Page That Doesn't Kill Your Conversions

The landing page is where people go after they click your link. Most people build landers that are cluttered, confusing, and full of popups. Those landers bleed clicks. The lead lands, gets overwhelmed, and leaves.

Your lander needs to be short, clean, and obvious.

Three to four steps max. Tell the user exactly what to do, make it look legitimate, and get out of their way.

AI landing page builders can generate this in under five minutes. Host it on Netlify, Vercel, or Cloudflare, not on a free builder that will take your page down the second a video goes viral.

On the domain, keep it compliant. Never use a brand's full name. Instead of walmartgiftcard.com, use something like martreward.com or shopcard.co. Same idea, legally clean.
