# How to Use Google Maps to Find Paying Clients For Free.

there's no shortage of content online teaching you how to make money selling websites to small businesses. or building automation workflows. or running an ai agency etc.

youtube is flooded with it. twitter threads, courses, $2k cohorts, pick your poison.

but almost none of them tell you the part that actually matters: how do you find clients who are ready to pay for any of this?

that's where most people get stuck. they learn the skill, build the thing, set up the offer, and then sit there refreshing an empty inbox. cold DMs get ignored. cold calls go to voicemail. the skill isn't the problem. finding people who actually need it is.

this article is a practical, step-by-step guide for finding unlimited paying clients using google maps and a free scraper tool — no ads, no paid lead lists, no cold outreach gymnastics.

five steps. you can run the whole thing today.

---

## 01 — what makes a business a prospect

every local business is a bucket. they pour money in ads, signs, referrals trying to fill it with customers. but most of these buckets have holes:

leads come in and fall right through. the phone rings, nobody picks up. a customer finds them online, sees zero reviews, clicks the competitor. somebody visits their website and there's nowhere to book.

your job isn't to pour more water in. your job is to find the holes and plug them.

google maps is full of these businesses. the trick is knowing which holes to look for, so you can teach claude code to find them for you in step 03.

here are the four signals:

1. low review count: businesses ranking at the top have 200+ reviews. the ones buried below often have under 30. those are your prospects.
2. no website linked: no website = a massive hole. customers who want to research before calling have nowhere to land.
3. no responses to reviews: signals "nobody's home" to every future customer reading those reviews.
4. missing basics: no photos, no hours, no description. every empty field is a reason to scroll past.

two or more of these signals = prospect.

---

## 02 — scrape the list with instant data scraper

the fix is a free chrome extension called instant data scraper. it reads whatever's on the screen and dumps it into a clean csv. no signup, no api keys, no code.

install it, run your search on google maps, scroll to the bottom, click the extension icon, hit csv. open in google sheets.

what you get: name, address, phone number, website (if they have one), star rating, review count.

---

## 03 — feed the csv to claude code (filter + write emails)

two stages.

stage 1: filter the prospects. open claude code in the folder where you saved the csv. drop in a prompt to read the csv, score each row 0-4 based on the four signals, visit websites to check review responses, output a filtered prospects.csv with signal_score and signal_notes columns.

stage 2: make the calls. cold calls to qualified prospects book meetings at closer to 10% vs 1-2% for cold emails. three things in the 10-second opener: say their name, lead with the observation (not the offer), end with a question not a pitch.

stage 3: write the email fallback. same claude code session - read prospects.csv, write a short cold email tailored to each business based on its signal_notes. lowercase, friendly, lead with one specific observation, offer to share a free breakdown, never ask for a call.

---

## 04 — handle the three objections, book the meeting

"i'm not interested." — agree with them, then bring it back to the observation.
"just send me an email." — agree to send it but get a small commitment first.
"how much?" — don't answer yet. "depends on the setup, easiest way is i hop on a quick 15-minute call this week, does tomorrow at 10 work?"

booking: "does tomorrow at 10am work, or would later in the week be better?" confirm the time and date out loud, get their email right then, send the calendar invite within 5 minutes.

---

## 05 — close the meeting, deliver the work

show them the gap (screen-share their google listing), show them the fix (one-page website / review automation / ai receptionist), ask for the sale: "so this is what i'd build for you. want me to put it together this week?" stop talking. let the silence sit.

if yes → invoice within the hour, start the same day, send a progress update within 48 hours.
if "let me think about it" → "what would you need to know to decide today?"
if no → keep it warm, email them again in six months.

finish the job. ask for a testimonial. ask for two referrals.
