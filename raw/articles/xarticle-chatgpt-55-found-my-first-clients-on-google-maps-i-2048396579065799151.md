You take their business name, their services from the Google Maps profile and their best customer reviews then give ChatGPT this prompt:

```

- Hero header with their main service and years of experience
```

## Part 2 - Reputation management: $300-800 per month per client

Every business on Google Maps with under 4.2 stars is actively losing customers since studies show 94% of consumers avoid a business after reading negative reviews and a restaurant going from 3.8 to 4.3 stars increases revenue by an average of 9%.

Your service is simple - you monitor their Google Maps reviews, respond to every review using ChatGPT and implement a system to generate more positive reviews from happy customers while the business owner does absolutely nothing.

The prompt that generates perfect review responses:

```
You are a professional reputation manager for [Business Name],
a [type of business] in [city].

Write a professional, warm and personal response to this Google review:
[paste the review]

Guidelines:
- Thank them by first name if mentioned
- Address any specific complaint directly and professionally
- Offer to make it right if negative
- Under 150 words, sound human not corporate
- End with an invitation to return or contact directly
```

ChatGPT writes a response that sounds genuinely human in 30 seconds and you do this for every review across all your clients every morning spending a total of 20-30 minutes for 5 clients. 

Businesses that actively request reviews after good service consistently average 4.5+ stars within 90 days and that's exactly the transformation you're selling for $300-800 per month per client which at 10 clients gives you $3,000-8,000 in pure recurring revenue.

## Part 3 - Cold outreach on autopilot: 500 leads per day

Instead of finding clients one by one you build a machine that finds them, qualifies them and reaches out automatically through three steps that ChatGPT handles completely.

1. First step - scrape Google Maps using tools like Outscraper or PhantomBuster which pull business names, emails, phone numbers, ratings and review counts from any Google Maps search in any city and give you a spreadsheet of 200 businesses in 10 minutes including their full contact information.

2.  Second step - automatic qualification where you give ChatGPT the spreadsheet and ask it to identify the highest-priority targets with ratings between 3.5 and 4.2, more than 20 reviews, no website or an outdated URL in categories like HVAC, plumbing, electrical and roofing.

3. Third step - personalized outreach at scale where for each qualified target ChatGPT writes a cold email that references their specific situation:

```
Write a cold email to [Business Name], an HVAC company in [City]
with a 3.9 star rating and 47 Google reviews but no website.

The email should reference their specific rating and review count,
calculate how many customers they're probably losing to competitors
with better online presence, offer a free website preview built
specifically for their business and sound like it's from a real person
not a marketing agency. Under 150 words with one clear call to action.
```

The email lands in their inbox and reads like someone actually looked at their business specifically  because ChatGPT did - and personalized cold outreach converts 3-8x better than generic templates.
