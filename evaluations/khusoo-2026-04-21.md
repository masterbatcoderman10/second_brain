# Khusoo — Niche-Specificity Evaluation (2026-04-21)

Evaluated via the [[niche-specificity-digital-product]] framework by [[dickie-bush]].

## Specificity Score: 11/15

## Strengths

- **Niche down:** ✅ Prayer-focused Muslims who have phone distraction problems — very specific audience
- **Single problem:** ✅ One problem, one job to be done — protecting the pre-prayer window
- **Outcome stated:** ✅ "never rushing prayers again" — clear outcome
- **Specificity of context:** ✅ Urban, 18-35, English-speaking — specific user profile
- **Emotional pain:** ✅ The guilt/regret of missing prayer is a strong motivator

## Gaps (4 identified)

1. **Missing outcome list** — the page mentions "never rushing prayers" but doesn't enumerate specific measurable outcomes (prayers protected per week, reduced screen time before salah, etc.)
2. **Unconfirmed problem list** — while the problem is stated, there's no explicit "problem list" section documenting exactly what users searched for / complained about
3. **Niche too wide** — "prayer-focused Muslims" covers 1.8B people; consider narrowing further: e.g., "young professionals in the West who use prayer apps and feel guilty about phone scrolling"
4. **Credibility undocumented** — the subscription/sadaqah framing is novel and bold, but no evidence it resonates; needs user interviews or survey validation before committing

## Verdict

**Revise before validation.** The core idea is solid and the specificity score (11/15) is above the threshold, but all 4 gaps should be addressed before spending engineering time. Specifically: sharpen the ICP to a subset of the broader Muslim demographic, add measurable outcome language, and get 5-10 real user interviews to validate the sadaqah framing.
