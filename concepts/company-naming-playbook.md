# Company Naming Playbook

Company naming framework from Scott Bair (co-founder of Lunour branding agency), adapted from Lexicon Branding's methodology ($75K–$200K per project).

## Core Framework

### The Three Jobs of a Great Name

1. **Gets attention** — stands out in a sea of noise
2. **Holds attention** — easy to process, say, remember (processing fluency)
3. **Surprises** — creates a "wait, what?" moment

Most founders go safe and descriptive → invisible zone. You want the **tension zone**: half the room loves it, half questions it. Energy is the signal.

### The Tension Zone vs. Invisible Zone

| Zone | Reaction | Result |
|------|----------|--------|
| Tension | Half love, half hate | Memorable, compounding asset |
| Invisible | "Yeah, that's fine" | Friction daily |

Case: Intel engineers wanted "ProChip." Andy Grove chose "Pentium" despite internal pushback. "A polarizing name has energy." Result: one of the most recognized processor names ever.

## The Benefit Ladder

Name the ultimate benefit, not the product feature:

```
Feature → Functional benefit → Emotional benefit → Ultimate benefit
```

Example: Project management tool → "Clarity" (feeling everything is handled). "Clarity" gives 100x more creative fuel than "project management."

## The Process

### Phase 1: Map the Landscape
- List 20 competitors + naming patterns
- Identify what's overused → off-limits
- Define "we're NOT going there" boundaries

### Phase 2: Find the Ultimate Benefit
- Write benefit ladder for your company
- Interview customers: "How do you FEEL after using this?" (not what it does)
- Identify ultimate benefit in 1-2 words

### Phase 3: The Treasure Hunt
- Target 300+ candidates (Lexicon does 2,000+)
- Explore: etymology, Latin/Greek roots, 5+ adjacent fields, word fragments
- Use AI for quantity; apply human judgment for quality
- Use "approximate thinking" — sit between absurd and obvious

### Phase 4: The Tension Test
- Share top 40-60 names one-on-one with 5-10 people
- Ask "what does this make you feel?" — feelings, not opinions
- Polarizing = signal. Don't dismiss it.

### Phase 5: Final Evaluation
Score on: distinctiveness, processing fluency, emotional resonance, energy, longevity, domain availability.

**Remarkable beats balanced.** Pick the one scoring 9-10 on distinctiveness and energy, even if 6 elsewhere.

## Sound Symbolism

- Hard consonants (K, T, P): strong, precise
- Soft sounds (L, M, S): smooth, gentle
- V: "most daring letter in English"

Case: Zeit → Vercel (from versatile, accelerate, excel). V adds confidence. $9.3B valuation.

## AI in Naming

AI generates candidates in minutes — competitive advantage shifted from **generation to judgment**. AI is terrible at feeling, gut reactions, tension. Use AI for 200-300 candidate batch, then apply human judgment. **Note:** AI names are initially bland — do benefit ladder work first for direction.

## When to Rename

Pre-Series B: rename is a PR moment, not an equity loss. Customers know your product, not your name. Signs you need to rename: unspellable, always explaining it, describes what you did vs. what you are, domain disaster, cringing when you say it.
