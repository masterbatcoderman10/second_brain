# ai-ugc-behavioral-biases

Framework by [[adriansolarzz]] applying behavioral science to AI UGC content creation. The thesis: viral AI UGC operators understand, at a behavioral science level, *why* certain content stops scrolls, holds attention, triggers emotion, and drives action — and they engineer those mechanisms systematically rather than relying on intuition.

The framework stacks multiple cognitive biases across campaign architecture layers, activating the bias most relevant to each viewer decision point.

## Biases in the Framework

### Scroll Battle (Attention Capture)

- **Novelty Bias** — brain allocates disproportionate attention to stimuli that deviate from niche norms; tactic is a monthly audit of top 30 reels, identifying elements appearing in fewer than 3
- **Orienting Response** — involuntary attention pause from unexpected stimulus (micro-imperfection in first 2 seconds: single-frame focus shift, ambient sound, camera micro-movement)
- **Information Gap Theory** — curiosity felt as discomfort from a knowledge gap; hooks with specific counterintuitive claims create open loops that scroll reflex cannot override

### Belonging (For-THEM Feeling)

- **In-Group Bias** — persona-specific hook language using exact phrases from customer reviews/social comments creates "this person gets it" response before any product claim
- **Pratfall Effect** — 1 vulnerability moment in opening 20 seconds humanizes and reduces psychological distance from polished promotion
- **Mere Exposure Effect** — multi-account portfolio delivers 3+ doses of consistent visual/stylistic content in one session, accelerating familiarity-based trust

### Emotional Engagement (Feel Something)

- **Empathy Gap** — present-tense pain sections induce the emotional state being described, placing viewer inside the experience rather than outside it; "I am exhausted" vs "I used to be exhausted"
- **Loss Aversion** — framing ongoing cost of inaction in concrete terms (irreversibility of time lost) activates loss aversion ~2× more powerfully than equivalent gain framing
- **FOMO Effect** — framing results as something a *specific accessible peer group* is already experiencing ("brands crossing $100k/mo are already doing this") outperforms hypothetical framing

### Conversion (Feeling → Acting)

- **Anchoring Effect** — stating cost of failed alternatives before product price makes the price appear smaller regardless of absolute value; "I spent $1,800 on gym memberships over 3 years" anchors $120 product as rational
- **Commitment and Consistency** — quiz funnel questions elicit small commitments about situation; arriving at recommendation screen means rejecting the recommendation requires rejecting self-answers
- **Zeigarnik Effect** — 2-3 open loops engineered at section transitions carry viewer through each transition with active motivation; loops at pain section end, mechanism bridge end, and CTA

### Sharing (Organic Distribution)

- **Identity Signaling Bias** — content designed to signal sharer's group membership or values generates DM shares informational content cannot approach
- **Reciprocity Principle** — pure how-to format delivering genuine utility before any CTA activates reciprocity; "warm traffic from people who feel they owe the brand something before being asked"
- **Narrative Transportation** — deep narrative engagement reduces counterarguing; specificity of persona research determines depth of transportation; exact customer language in pain sections produced 3× higher save rate

## Full Campaign Bias Stack

The compounding effect comes from stacking biases across the complete campaign architecture:

1. **First frame** → novelty bias + orienting response + contrast effect (3 biases via single production decision)
2. **Hook** → information gap + FOMO (open loop + peer group framing)
3. **Pain section** → empathy gap + loss aversion + in-group bias (emotional immersion + concrete cost + "gets me")
4. **Mechanism bridge** → Zeigarnik effect (open loop) + anchoring (failed alternative cost)
5. **Transformation** → awe response + narrative transportation (inside the narrative)
6. **CTA** → reciprocity + commitment/consistency (logical continuation vs sales transaction)
7. **Comment section** → social proof cascade via pinned comment

## Key Claims

- Skincare client reel: 3.1M views in 4 days; "only thing different was the script"
- $800,000+ generated through viral reels (self-reported by adriansolarzz)
- Postpartum fitness campaign: loss-frame sentence in pain section improved watch-through rate and conversion vs version without it
- Narrative specificity campaign: exact customer language in pain section produced 3× higher save rate vs generic-language version
