# App Store Packaging

## Definition

The set of visible storefront elements — icon, screenshots, onboarding, paywall, and user flow — that determine whether a user downloads an app. Apps stuck at low MRR have a **packaging problem**, not a traffic problem. The 5 packaging elements must work together to convert scrolling users into downloading, using, and paying users.

## The 5 Rules (Viktor Seraleev)

### 1. Icon

- **First impression** — Users decide to tap or scroll in milliseconds
- **Requirements:** Simple design, conveys app idea, polished and professional
- **Thumb test:** Must read clearly at 60×60 App Store search size
- **Bold color, one clear subject**
- **ChatGPT can generate mood boards** to explore icon styles rapidly

### 2. Screenshots

- **Tell a story, don't teach the app** — Users should understand the app before tapping "Get"
- **First 3 screenshots are seen from search results on iOS** — most important real estate
- **Gifmo example:** Paint with recognizable memes → tease template picker → show GIF video editor
- **Pattern:** Recognition → teaser → payoff
- Stylized real screens with large readable text

### 3. Onboarding

- **3-4 screens max** — paint the outcome, not the process
- **Don't teach how to use the app**
- **One action per step**
- **Reinforce messaging from App Store screenshots**
- **Goal:** Get users to the "aha" moment as fast as possible
- **Videos in onboarding** outperform static images
- **75% of Viktor's revenue** comes from paywall placement before users access the app

### 4. Paywall

- **Place right after the value moment** — no hidden close button, no fake urgency, no price tricks
- **Clear offer, clear value**
- **Pricing tactics that work:**
  - 3-day, 7-day, or 30-day free trial
  - Weekly pricing: $5.99-7.99/week
  - Annual: $29.99-59.99/year
  - Skip lifetime deals — drive recurring revenue
  - Annual pricing highlights the discount ($312.34 vs $29.99 = 90% off)
- **Math for conversion:** If 1% of users see paywall and 10% convert → need 1,000 downloads per paying customer

### 5. User Flow

- **One screen, one action, clear path forward**
- Every extra tap loses users
- Every decision point is a bounce risk
- Remove anything that makes users stop and think

## The $250 → $5K MRR Journey

The underlying case study: an app stuck at $250-300/month MRR for years, then following Viktor's packaging changes over ~1 year → $5K/month organic traffic only. The mechanism is not more traffic — it's better conversion of existing traffic through packaging.
