# Startup Launch Framework

A manual-first, discovery-driven methodology for launching a SaaS or AI company from zero, sourced from [[adam-robinson]]'s experience building 3 software companies to $35M+ combined ARR.

## Core Thesis

Most founders fail because they fall in love with their idea instead of their customers' problems. They build for 12–18 months before talking to anyone. The framework inverts this: **validate before building, stay manual as long as possible**.

## Discovery Phase

**50 discovery calls minimum** before writing any code. The only goal: find people who will prepay for something you haven't built yet.

## Pre-Payment Validation Techniques

1. **Personal network first**: LinkedIn outreach to people matching your ICP, offering a $100 gift card for feedback
2. **Paid advisory roles**: Director-level people in your ICP as paid advisors
3. **Industry events**: Approach influential people with a hand-drawn product sketch and ask what would make them buy
4. **On-call during discovery**: 6-question framework:
   - Do you understand the problem I'm trying to solve?
   - Do you have this problem?
   - How are you solving it today?
   - Would you pay for this solution if I could get it to you tomorrow?
   - What else would it need for you to pay for it?
   - I'm thinking of charging X per month. Would you pay that?

## Staying Manual

> "If it takes more than 4–6 weeks, I'm building too much."

With RB2B, Adam's team manually sent spreadsheets over email for **5 months** before putting up a website. Staying manual:
- Keeps you close to customers
- Enables 10x faster iteration than competitors automating everything
- Is the **unfair advantage** — a competitor with more resources can't out-manual you

## 3 PMF Signals

All 3 must be present before scaling beyond the discovery loop:

1. **At least one referral per week** from paying customers
2. **Customer distress if product removed** — they'd be genuinely upset
3. **Organic growth without any marketing** — no paid ads, no outreach push

> "Nothing else will work without it."

## Build In Public

Post on LinkedIn from day one. Share the journey — wins, failures, pivots. By the time outreach begins, people already know who you are.
