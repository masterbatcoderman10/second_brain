# clear-graphics-yc-landing-page-framework

Landing page structure used by top YC-funded companies (Resend, Chatbase, Artisan, Deel, Vanta) — distilled into a 7-pattern framework by [[clear-graphics]].

## The 7-Pattern Framework

### Pattern 1: Hero — headline + real screenshot
- Headline under 10 words
- Actual product UI screenshot (not mockups or illustrations)
- Immediately answers "what does this product look like"
- Builds trust by proving the product exists and is polished

### Pattern 2: Social proof within first scroll
- Customer logos or usage metrics visible within 500px of page top
- Examples: "trusted by the best teams", "10k businesses worldwide", "40,000+ companies"

### Pattern 3: One CTA repeated consistently
- Single primary action: "get started" / "start free trial" / "book demo"
- Appears in hero, after first feature section, AND at bottom
- No competing actions in the hero section

### Pattern 4: Founder photos at early stage
- 4/5 YC companies had visible founder/team photos
- Critical trust signal for investors and early adopters
- At later stages shifts to investor logos and press mentions
- "Faceless startups feel risky and lifeless"

### Pattern 5: Pricing visible and simple
- 4/5 had pricing on the page or one click away
- Free tier or starting price shown even for enterprise products
- Hidden pricing kills conversion for developer tools and SMBs

### Pattern 6: 3-6 feature sections max
- Each feature gets its own section: visual + headline + 1-2 sentences
- "Fewer features presented well DESTROYS a long feature list nobody reads"

### Pattern 7: Page ends with action, not information
- Final CTA block with benefit-driven headline + button
- Target: warmest traffic (those who scrolled to bottom)

## The Purchase Decision Sequence

The framework maps to how the brain processes purchase decisions:

> what is this → who uses it → what problem does it solve → how does it work → what does it cost → who built it → should i try it
