# JARVIS Obsidian Second Brain

A compounding knowledge and content system built inside Obsidian, powered by Claude Code. Unlike typical note vaults that accumulate disconnected captures, JARVIS actively connects ideas, generates content briefs, and learns from performance data to produce increasingly useful output over time.

## Core Architecture

- **00-INBOX** — Zero-friction raw capture zone; sort later
- **01-CAPTURES/** — Organized by *type* (observations, reactions, patterns, questions, numbers), not topic — type organization creates collisions between ideas that topic silos would keep apart
- **02-CONNECTIONS** — Synthesized insights from two+ captures; the source of original content
- **03-BRIEFS** — Production-ready content briefs: hook written, closer written, source notes linked
- **04-PUBLISHED** — Archived content with performance data (impressions, bookmarks, engagement)
- **05-CLAUDE/** — Claude Code working directory; contains CLAUDE.md and skills

## Key Principles

1. **Type organization over topic organization** — notes about AI strategy and psychology land in the same `patterns/` folder and find each other automatically
2. **CLAUDE.md is the most important file** — tells Claude Code who you are, how your vault is organized, and your exact writing voice; vague CLAUDE.md produces vague output
3. **Four core skills**: Process Inbox, Weekly Connections, Generate Brief, Write Content
4. **Daily 20-minute ritual**: 5min capture → 5min process → 5min connect → 5min brief
5. **Performance loop**: Every published piece's data feeds back into the vault; monthly session surfaces what to write next based on your actual audience's behavior

## Why It Compounds

After 30 days: smarter than day one (connections accumulate)
After 90 days: surfaces surprising relationships between notes months apart
After 6 months: knows your content better than any analytics dashboard because it reads ideas + results together, not just numbers
