# Generalist Entrepreneurship

The philosophy that individuals with multiple interests (polymaths, multipotentialites) are not scattered or unfocused — they are positioned for the strongest possible competitive advantage in the digital economy, if they have a "vessel" to channel their interests into meaningful, monetizable work.

## Thesis

Specialization was optimized for the Industrial Age (pin factories: 20 pins/day → 48,000/day via division of labor). Schools were designed to produce punctual, obedient factory workers. But this model makes people "stupid and dependent" — dependent on employers, on formal credentials, on a single skill.

The internet and AI have recreated the conditions of the Renaissance. The printing press collapsed the cost of knowledge, enabling Da Vinci, Michelangelo. The internet has collapsed the cost of distribution, enabling the independent creator.

## Three Ingredients

1. **Self-education** — directing one's own learning rather than relying on formal credentials
2. **Self-interest** — following one's actual interests (not what corporations assign); the only alternative is serving others' interests
3. **Self-sufficiency** — refusing to outsource judgment, learning, and agency

From these three, the generalist emerges naturally: self-interest motivates learning; self-education enables sovereignty; self-sufficiency clarifies what actually serves you.

## Generalist vs Specialist

| | Specialist | Generalist |
|--|--|--|
| Value model | One skill, high depth | Many skills, compounding connections |
| Risk | Disposable if task is automated | Ideas across domains create unique perspective |
| Opportunity | Narrow roles | Sees intersections specialists cannot |
| Market position | Replaceable | Irreplaceable moat: a unique opinion |

The generalist advantage: "ideas across domains complement each other and create a unique way of viewing the world, which allows them to catch novel ideas from the aether and translate them into market value."

## The Vessel

The missing piece is not more learning — it is a **vessel**: a way to channel multiple interests into one coherent business. The vessel must allow you to:
- Pursue your goals (brand)
- Teach what you learn (content)
- Help others achieve goals faster (product)

## Two Paths to Monetization

**Path 1 — Skill-Based**: Learn one marketable skill → teach it via content → sell a product related to it. Limitation: one-dimensional, creates a second 9-to-5.

**Path 2 — Development-Based** (preferred): Turn yourself into the customer avatar. You pursue your goals → this validates the usefulness of your offer → you help the past version of yourself. This path subsumes Path 1 (you still need marketable skills) but starts from genuine interest rather than chasing profit.
