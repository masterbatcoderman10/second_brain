# Topical Authority (SEO)

## Definition

Topical authority is an SEO strategy for making a site look like the most complete and credible resource on a narrow subject. In [[noel-ceta]]'s framing, this comes from focused topic coverage, explicit semantic relationships between pages, strong internal linking, and visible expertise signals rather than from domain age alone.

## Core framework from the source

The thread presents a seven-step sequence:

1. Choose a narrow niche.
2. Map the topic landscape.
3. Build pillar-cluster architecture.
4. Create comprehensive content.
5. Establish internal linking.
6. Demonstrate expertise signals.
7. Earn topical backlinks.

This makes topical authority less of a single ranking trick and more of a coordinated editorial and site-architecture system.

## Operating mechanics

### 1. Narrow niche first
The source argues that newer sites should start with very constrained scope (for example, "B2B SaaS content marketing" instead of "marketing") so authority compounds within one semantic neighborhood before expansion.

### 2. Topic-map coverage
The operating unit is not one page but a full topic map. The thread recommends identifying 50-100 subtopics through keyword tools, related searches, competitor analysis, and community discussions.

### 3. Pillar-cluster structure
A central pillar page covers the main topic broadly, while 10-15 cluster pages go deep on specific subtopics and link back to the pillar. This is structurally adjacent to [[programmatic-seo]], but the emphasis here is not page volume alone — it is coherent topic coverage and reinforcement.

### 4. Comprehensive content standards
The source recommends original research, specific advice, visual aids, examples, expert contributions, and regular updates. In practice, this overlaps with some of the content-shaping discipline seen in [[prompt-engineering-patterns]] and with the information-depth requirements that later support [[llm-seo]].

### 5. Internal-linking discipline
The thread treats internal linking as a ranking mechanism, not an afterthought:
- pillar pages link to all clusters
- cluster pages link back to the pillar
- related clusters link to each other
- each new piece links to 3-5 older pieces

This creates a topical relevance web that distributes authority through the site.

### 6. Expertise signals and topical backlinks
The remaining layers are public proof and external validation: author bios, citations, case studies, expert contributors, and backlinks from niche-relevant sites.

### Likely but unverified
- The claimed example results: outranking DR 70+ sites, 47 first-page keywords, 12K monthly visits in six months, and industry-leader citations.
- The broad claim that topic expertise outweighs domain age in the generalized way the thread states.

### Speculative synthesis
- In this wiki's taxonomy, topical authority appears best modeled as the site-structure substrate beneath [[programmatic-seo]] and a useful precursor to [[llm-seo]].

## Why it matters

This concept is useful because it gives a principled way to turn a new niche site into a dense semantic cluster instead of a loose set of articles. The practical lesson is that SEO authority is being framed here as graph quality plus editorial discipline, not just backlinks or domain age.
