# Singularity Scalers

## Overview

A concept from Antonio Linares's investment framework — companies experiencing **exponential free cash flow (FCF) growth** driven by AI-enabled capabilities compounding faster than competitors can replicate.

## Key Characteristics

- Revenue/FCF growth curve is non-linear (exponential, not linear)
- AI capabilities compound: more data → better model → better product → more users → more data
- Network effects layer on top of the AI flywheel
- Traditional DCF models misprice these because they're designed for linear growth

## Examples (from the source)

- AMD at $4.2 (AI GPU demand)
- Tesla at $13 (AI-assisted autonomous driving)
- Palantir at $7 (AI data analytics)

## Relationship to [[ontology-velocity-investment-framework]]

Singularity Scalers are one of four investment lenses in the Ontology Velocity framework. The "singularity" refers to the moment when an AI company's compounding flywheel becomes self-reinforcing enough that competitor response becomes nearly impossible.
