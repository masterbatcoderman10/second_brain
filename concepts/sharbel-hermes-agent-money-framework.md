# Sharbel's Hermes Agent Money Framework

## Core Thesis

An AI agent that doesn't make money is just a chatbot. The agent is not the business — it is the leverage behind the business. The framework focuses on **paid loops**: small, self-contained workflows that produce a monetizable output on a repeating schedule.

The key inversion: do not start by asking "what can my agent automate?" Start by asking "what useful outcome can my agent produce every week that someone would pay for?"

**[[monetization]] principle:** Sell the output, not the agent. Businesses pay for painful bottlenecks solved — leads, research, alerts, reports, content — not for "an AI agent."

## The 5 Starter Offers

### 1. Weekly Niche Intelligence Brief

Monitor sources (AI tools, crypto, real estate, hiring, funding, competitor launches, regulatory updates) within a chosen niche. Deliver a curated weekly brief: 10 things that changed, why they matter, what to do next.

**Target buyer:** founder, investor, creator, agency, recruiter, trader.

**Acquisition:** One free sample → first paying customer.

### 2. Lead Research and Outreach Prep

Find target accounts, research companies, identify buying signals, summarize opportunities, draft personalized outreach messages, organize in CRM.

**Key framing:** Sell supervised leverage, not autonomy. "I deliver 50 researched leads per week with custom angles" beats "I built an AI sales agent."

### 3. Competitor Monitoring

Watch pricing pages, product pages, job listings, social posts, ads, newsletters, changelogs, funding news, app updates. Deliver weekly "what changed / why it matters / what to do" reports.

**Observation:** The best agent businesses won't all look futuristic. Some will look boring — and boring is why they work.

### 4. Founder Content Engine

Track trends, study outlier posts, collect examples, summarize news. Produce weekly: 5 post ideas, 3 founder stories, 10 hooks, 1 long-form draft, 1 video script, 1 newsletter outline.

**Key framing:** Not "AI writes your posts" (too cheap). Instead: "I run your content research system every week so you never start from a blank page."

### 5. Market Alert Agent

Monitor markets, APIs, wallets, news, filings, tickers, or specific events. Deliver structured alerts: what happened, why it matters, trigger conditions, pre-action checklist.

**Progression:** Alerts → Simulations → Paper trading → Strict limits. Never start with real capital.

## The 7-Day Plan

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 1 | Pick one painful outcome (leads, competitor intel, niche brief, market alerts, or content) |
| 2 | Build the Hermes workflow manually — produce the output once with the agent's help |
| 3 | Turn the workflow into a repeatable checklist (sources, rules, format, delivery, quality bar) |
| 4 | Create one polished sample deliverable worth forwarding |
| 5 | Send to 10 people who have the problem — pitch the result, not the agent |
| 6 | Charge for version two (even $50 proves someone values the output) |
| 7 | Automate the repeated parts into Hermes skills, cron jobs, scripts, and delivery workflows |

## Why Hermes Fits This Framework

Hermes can work autonomously (tools, web search, file access, scripts, APIs, memory, skills, cron jobs, Telegram delivery). Money-making workflows are rarely a single prompt — they are **loops**: Monitor → Filter → Analyze → Package → Deliver → Repeat.

This loop structure is what separates a revenue-generating agent from a chatbot.
