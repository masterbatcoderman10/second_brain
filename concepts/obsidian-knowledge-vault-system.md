# Obsidian Knowledge Vault System

## Summary

A five-folder Obsidian vault (Inbox, Notes, Ideas, Projects, CLAUDE.md) fed by automated capture through N8N, with Claude as the intelligence layer that surfaces connections, writes daily briefs, and runs weekly synthesis. Built for founders who want a thinking partner, not a filing cabinet.

## Key Points

- **Core insight:** Most knowledge systems fail because they are designed for input, not output. Information that goes in but never comes back out is not a knowledge system — it is a graveyard with good folders.
- **The three failure modes:** Capture friction, no connection layer, no reason to return.
- **Four-layer architecture:** Capture → Pipeline (N8N) → Obsidian → Claude.
- **Five-folder structure:** Inbox, Notes, Ideas, Projects, CLAUDE.md. Simple intentionally — complex structures collapse under their own weight.
- **CLAUDE.md:** The most important file. Tells Claude who you are, what you are working on, and what you want from it. Update weekly.
- **Daily brief:** N8N automation runs every weekday at 6am. Claude reads inbox (last 24h) and notes (last 7 days), surfaces 3 connections, identifies 1 pattern, poses 1 question.
- **Weekly synthesis:** 15-minute session with Claude reviewing the entire vault, identifying emerging thesis, contradictions, knowledge gaps, and one highest-leverage action.
- **Compound effect:** At 6 months, you have a record of every belief held and changed, every question sat with and answered.
