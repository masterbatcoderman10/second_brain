# Perplexity Computer App Idea Research

A 5-step market research methodology for finding viable mobile app ideas using Perplexity Computer as the primary research tool. Replaces a week of manual research across X, TikTok, Sensor Tower, and the App Store.

## Overview

The method structures a single Perplexity session into a full app market research pipeline: discover revenue-generating indie creators, analyze existing app categories, validate trends on TikTok, score opportunities, and produce a shortlist with v1 feature recommendations.

## The 5 Steps

1. **Creator Research** — Find 5 indie creators on X sharing real revenue data ($10K–100K/mo). Verify claims against App Store ratings, review counts, and Sensor Tower. Tools and distribution channels documented per creator.
2. **App Market Analysis** — Identify 8–10 revenue-generating categories. Score top 3–5 apps per category on rating, revenue, downloads, update recency, and user complaints. Flag weak competitors.
3. **TikTok Trend Validation** — For each category, find demo videos and rank by view counts and viral format potential. Only keep categories with clear TikTok traction or an obvious viral demo format.
4. **Scoring (out of 20)** — Rank remaining ideas by:
   - Virality (V): Viral potential on TikTok, demo compelling in under 15 seconds
   - Build Difficulty (B): v1 buildable in 1–2 weeks by one developer with AI tools
   - Revenue Potential (R): Realistic monthly revenue ceiling for the category
   - Competition Gap (C): Weak competitors, unfilled feature/design/experience gap
5. **Deep Dive on Top 3** — For each: feature gap, target user profile, exact TikTok hook description, max 5-feature v1 scope, and one failure mode.

## Key Principles

- **Run twice a month.** App trends shift in 60 days — a gap today may be crowded tomorrow.
- **Reject obvious ideas.** If the top result is something everyone is already building, the market is saturated. Push the agent deeper.
- **Pair with App Store review mining.** After selecting an idea, run a second Perplexity prompt to read all 1-star and 2-star reviews of the top competitor — this becomes the feature roadmap.
