# 60 GitHub accounts every AI builder should follow

X Article by [[meta-alchemist]] posted 2026-05-03. A curated guide to 60 GitHub accounts mapped across the seven layers of the AI stack — foundational models, inference, agents, infra, and application layers. Each entry includes what the builder shipped, why it matters, and what a builder can take from their work to ship their own products.

The article argues most AI builders follow launch threads and demo clips but miss the source — the actual people building the field. The playbook: save the list, open two profiles per day for 30 days, research what they built deeply, and end with a working map of the AI world, repos worth cloning, and blue oceans to claim.

Key advice: read the design choices in README files (not just the code), spot the unfair advantage each builder had, run their code and ship something on top of it by Friday, treat their work as a starter kit not a finished product, and follow who they follow.
