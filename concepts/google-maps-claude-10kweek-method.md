# Google Maps + Claude $10k+/Week Method

Four-tool workflow (Google Maps → Claude → Lovable → Higgsfield) for pre-selling website services to local businesses at $10k+/week.

## Overview

The old playbook for selling services to local businesses was lead lists, cold calls, generic templates, and weeks of follow-up to land one client. This method flips that completely: walk into the first conversation already holding a finished version of what they would buy, custom built, branded to them, with a video showing it in motion.

The prospect does not need to imagine anything. They are looking at it. The decision becomes whether they want this specific thing, not whether they want to start a project.

## The Stack

> **Google Maps** finds local businesses with bad or missing websites.
> **Claude** writes personalized outreach and site briefs in one prompt batch.
> **Lovable** builds a working hosted landing page mockup in 5 minutes.
> **Higgsfield** generates a 10-second cinematic walkthrough video of the mockup.

## Step 1 — Find the Right Leads

Pick a niche where the owner is not technical and the website is critical to revenue. Best: roofers, landscapers, plumbers, fence installers, chimney repair, HVAC, dental practices, salons, law firms, real estate agents, photographers, event venues.

Open Google Maps and hunt. Type narrow queries ("cosmetic dentists in West Austin" not "dentists in Austin"). Skip the top 3–4 results — those are already crushing it. Look for the sweet spot:

- Established business, 5+ years on the map
- Low review count, under 50
- Either no website link or one built in 2014
- Solid reviews despite bad online presence

Aim for 25–30 leads from a single niche in one city. Feed to Claude to enrich and structure.

## Step 2 — Run the List Through Claude

One prompt, three deliverables per business:

1. **Diagnosis** (50 words): what is wrong with their current online presence and what revenue is leaking
2. **Site brief** (100 words): hero angle, key services, tone, CTA, one design differentiator
3. **Cold message** (under 70 words): opens with one specific observation about THIS business, references actual service or location, ends with soft ask to see mockup

Format as a table for CRM pasting.

## Step 3 — Build Mockups in Lovable

Do not build for all 30 leads — build for your top 5–8. Pick leads with the most specific diagnoses or highest before/after potential.

Lovable prompt template: specify audience, brand feel (3 adjectives), hero focus, sections in order, design details (color palette, mobile-first, subtle animations), tone. Avoid AI-looking gradients, generic stock photos, "Welcome to" headlines.

Hit publish. You now have a live URL.

## Step 4 — Generate Demo Videos in Higgsfield

Static screenshots get ignored. A 10-second video of their site in motion gets replied to.

Upload 3–5 screenshots from the Lovable mockup (hero, services, about, social proof, CTA). Prompt: slow zoom on hero (2s), smooth pan to services, gentle ease into about, end on CTA with soft fade. Style: premium, cinematic, modern editorial. Format: 9:16 vertical, 1080×1920.

Vertical format matters because most prospects open emails on their phones.

## Step 5 — Send the Outreach

Rule that breaks 90% of outreach: do not mention AI, Claude, Lovable, or any tool you used. The prospect cares about results, not your stack.

Cold message format:
```
Hey [first name], built you a quick site mockup based on what I saw on your Google profile.

[One specific observation that proves you actually looked.]

10-second walkthrough: [Higgsfield video link]
Full preview: [Lovable URL]

If it looks close to what you would want, happy to chat. If not, no worries.

[Your name]
```

Under 70 words. The video sells. The preview link proves you did the work. The soft close removes pressure.

Channel by niche:
- **Email**: most niches default
- **SMS**: contractors, trades, plumbers
- **Instagram DM**: salons, restaurants, visual businesses
- **LinkedIn**: law firms, financial services, B2B
- **Phone calls**: contractors, cleaners, older demographic

## What Closes the Deal

The mockup is the icebreaker. The Zoom call is what closes.

When a prospect replies positive, get them on a 10–15 minute Zoom. Walk through the mockup with them on screen. Ask what they would change. Quote them on the spot.

Close rate from positive reply: 30–50%.

## The Math

- Send 30 personalized sequences this weekend
- 10–15% reply rate → 3–4 positive replies
- 30–50% close rate → 1–2 deals per weekend

Monthly model: $500–1,000 new MRR every weekend × 2 weekends/month = $2,000–4,000 new MRR.

Six months = $12,000–24,000 monthly recurring.

One-time model: $2,500–10,000 cash per weekend.

## Time to Execute

- First batch (learning the tools): 4–6 hours for 5 polished mockups and videos
- Once dialed in: **80 minutes total** for a 30-prospect outreach sequence (5 min Claude, 30 min Lovable on top 5, 15 min Higgsfield, 30 min sending)
