# Paid Ads Agency Funnel

## Definition

A paid ads agency funnel is a structured B2B client acquisition system using paid social advertising (primarily Meta) to attract, qualify, and convert high-ticket clients. The concept, documented by [[george-clem]] analyzing Daniel Iles's Viral Coach agency, describes how agencies can scale to $40M+/year by running broad-target paid ads funnels with a single high-ticket offer and operationalized sales infrastructure.

## Key Principles

### 1. Broad TAM with One High-Ticket Offer

Agencies targeting a narrowly-defined avatar (e.g., "e-commerce brands doing $500k–$2M/month in a specific niche") face small addressable audiences (~30k–50k people) that exhaust quickly, causing CPMs and cost-per-call to climb.

By contrast, targeting all B2B business owners broadly (law firms, accounting firms, contractors, consultancies) provides a TAM of millions, enabling $100k+/month ad spend without audience fatigue — as long as the offer is flexible enough to appeal broadly.

**Trade-off principle**: small TAM + multiple price points vs. massive TAM + 1 high-ticket offer. Both can work; Viral Coach chose the second path.

### 2. Messaging Consistency Across Surfaces

Validated messaging should flow through every funnel surface:
- Ad hook → landing page headline → VSL opening → sales call framing

This is interchangeable marketing assets — the same core angle, repeated in different formats.

### 3. Creative Strategy: 30/30/30/10 Mix

For accounts spending $1,000+/day:
- **30%** direct offer ads (founder talking directly to camera)
- **30%** case study / testimonial ads
- **30%** educational top-of-funnel ads (UGC-style, podcast-format)
- **10%** wildcard creative (off-the-cuff, weird, doesn't look like an ad)

Below $1,000/day: start with direct offer ads only until you have a profitable winner.

**Primary text insight**: Once validated primary text copy works, stop iterating on it. 99% of creative testing energy should go into the visual ad creative itself.

### 4. Funnel Architecture: Two Variants

**VSL Funnel** (for warmer audiences):
- Headline: proven hook from best-performing ad
- Under 5-minute VSL addressing the "million views → revenue" objection
- Minimal below-VSL: logos, CTA button, application form, testimonials
- No FAQ, no "our process," no long copy distraction

**Short-Form Funnel** (for colder audiences or alternate brand faces):
- Proven hook headline + subheading + CTA + application form
- No VSL — reduces friction but requires stronger pre-call nurture

### 5. Three-Tier Lead Routing

Application form collects: name, email, phone, revenue, marketing spend, customer LTV, start timing.

Leads are scored into tiers:
- **Tier 1**: largest businesses, highest budgets, ready to start → senior closers
- **Tier 2**: mid-quality → junior closers or senior setters for pre-qualification
- **Tier 3**: lower-quality → setter follow-up sequences, long-term email nurture, newsletter

### 6. Three-Function Setter System

Setters (sales development reps) run three operations:
1. **Partial submit recovery**: call/text within 5 minutes of incomplete applications
2. **No-show recovery**: call/text daily for up to 5 days after cancellations
3. **Booking confirmation**: confirm meetings, surface discovery context, quality-check borderline leads

This recovers 20–30% of bookings most agencies lose silently.

### 7. Pre-Call Nurture

Three-component sequence between booking and call:
1. **Email nurture** (5–9 emails): value, objection handling, offer reinforcement; 50–70% open rates indicate well-built sequences
2. **Personal outreach** via WhatsApp/iMessage: closer or setter reaches out directly to build rapport
3. **SDR confirmation calls**: verify attendance, gather prep context
