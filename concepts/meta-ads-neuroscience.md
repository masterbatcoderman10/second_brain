# Meta Ads Neuroscience

A behavioral neuroscience framework for Meta (Facebook/Instagram) advertising that explains why certain ads create compulsion to act while others get ignored. Source: X Article by [[ig-claims]] (~14,675 chars, May 2026).

## Core Frameworks

### Dopamine Trigger Framework

The brain releases dopamine not when receiving a reward, but when *anticipating* one. Meta ads exploit this via **anticipation gaps**:

- **Setup**: show the problem (e.g., "you're overpaying $40k/year in taxes")
- **Tease**: hint at the solution (e.g., "there's a 3-entity structure most CPAs miss")
- **Gap**: create information asymmetry (e.g., "watch this 18-minute breakdown")
- **Payoff**: promise resolution on the other side of the click

Implementation: never fully explain in the ad copy — mystery outperforms explanation. Show the result (screenshot of "$43k saved") not the mechanism.

### Social Proof Loops (Mirror Neurons)

Mirror neurons fire when observing others' actions, enabling simulation of their experience. Effective social proof requires specificity:

- Weak: "this changed my life" — too vague to simulate
- Strong: "I was spending $84k/year in taxes as an S-corp, restructured using their framework, now I pay $51k — saved $33k in year one"

Specificity activates multiple neural pathways simultaneously: numerical cognition, reward anticipation, loss aversion satisfaction, and social belonging.

### Regret-Based Selling (Loss Aversion)

Humans feel losses 2–3x more intensely than equivalent gains. Negative framing outperforms positive framing:

- Positive: "save $30k on taxes this year"
- Negative: "you're losing $30k annually by using the wrong structure"

**Regret amplification**: layer in future-self regret by showing compounding cost of inaction:
> "if you're overpaying $30k this year and you don't fix it, that's $30k lost this year, $60k over two years, $150k over five years"

### Price Anchoring (Relative Value Perception)

The brain processes relative value, not absolute value. A $5,000 service feels expensive in isolation but cheap when anchored against:
- Current losses ($40k/year makes $5k feel like 12.5% of savings)
- Alternatives (hiring a CFO at $150k/year makes $5k feel like 3.3% of that cost)
- Competition (show expensive option before revealing your price)
