# 4-Beat Short-Form Ad Structure

## Definition

A four-stage framework for converting attention into action in 15-second AI-generated video ads. Each beat occupies a fixed time window and serves a distinct cognitive function. The structure is designed for direct-response performance marketing on short-form platforms (TikTok, Reels, Shorts, X).

## The four beats

| Beat | Time | Job | Examples |
|------|------|-----|----------|
| **Hook** | 0–2s | Pattern break — force a pause | unexpected visual, bold claim, before moment, curiosity shot |
| **Problem** | 2–5s | Make the pain obvious in one moment | messy hair, slow workflow, bad result |
| **Proof** | 5–12s | Show the product working — don't explain it | transformation, demo step, output reveal, clear after |
| **CTA** | 12–15s | One action, one line | "Try it today", "Get started", "Watch the full demo" |

> The article's central claim: **if an ad has no proof moment, it is content — not a commercial.**

## The 3 commercial formats

The source identifies three formats [[seedance-2-0]] is particularly suited for:

1. **Before → After** — fastest to make, easiest to understand. Best for beauty, fitness, editing tools, productivity, AI tools.
2. **Demo in 3 steps** — great for products people don't understand instantly. Best for apps, platforms, SaaS, AI tools, workflows.
3. **Reaction ads** — show result first, then reaction. Best for anything visual: video, design, AI generations, transformations.

## Core operating thesis

The source's thesis is **creative iteration beats creative perfection**. The advantage of [[seedance-2-0]] is not that it makes one perfect ad — it makes generating five good variants fast enough to run systematic performance testing. The winner gets scaled; the losers get killed.

The reference pack discipline (1 identity anchor + 1 style anchor + 1 composition anchor + optional motion anchor) is what separates AI video that looks like a demo from video that looks like a commercial.
