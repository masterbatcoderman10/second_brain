# Services-as-Software

A solo-operator business model where one person sells business owners an outcome (more leads, more meetings, more revenue) and uses AI to automate 90% of the delivery underneath. The client pays for the result, not the agent stack. Documented by [[alex-vacca]] who scaled ColdIQ to $6.47M ARR and the aiagency.io accelerator to 287 members using this model.

## Core Thesis

One person can now run an AI services business that generates the revenue a 30-person agency used to need. The cost of running AI delivery infrastructure keeps falling every quarter, collapsing the human-bound cost structure that historically kept service margins low.

## Three Reasons This Is the Highest-Leverage Creation Vehicle in 2026

1. **Tap existing cash flow.** Product businesses must manufacture demand. Service businesses route demand that already exists. Millions of business owners doing $500K–$5M/year will write a check today if you can put more revenue in their pocket.

2. **Margins now look like software.** AI collapsed the human-delivery cost. Solo operators run closer to 80% margins because the bulk of work runs through agents. Almost everything you make is profit.

3. **Risk is near zero.** No inventory, no warehouse, no team, no fundraising. A laptop, a domain edge, and the willingness to do outbound is the entire requirement list.

## The Collapsed Cost Structure

The work that used to require a team of five and thousands in monthly cost now runs through one person and a stack of AI subscriptions. Headcount required to deliver a working go-to-market campaign dropped by roughly an order of magnitude in two years.

There is a brief window where solo-operator unit economics dominate incumbent unit economics. Incumbents will eventually deploy the same AI, but their cost structure, hierarchy, and compensation models are calcified around the old assumption.

## The Lindy Bet on Outbound

Cold outreach has been working since at least 1995 — 30 years of compounding evidence that humans respond to well-targeted messages from strangers. The "AI agency" category is 18 months old. Spectators who declare "AI killed cold email" have not run a campaign in 2026.

Volume is not the lever. The difference between 10,000 emails booking nothing and 200 emails booking 15 meetings is precision: right ICP, right offer, right intent signal, right copy.

## How to Start

1. **Niche and offer first.** The niche is the type of business you'll help. The offer is what you actually sell them. Lead generation is the easiest opener because no business says no to more clients.

2. **Pilot pricing: $2K–$3K/month.** Land first clients and prove capability. Then raise rates.

3. **12-week roadmap:**
   - Weeks 1–3: Foundation — pick niche, map buyer, write offer in one sentence
   - Weeks 4–6: Build and launch — productize service, one-page site, first outbound sequences
   - Weeks 7–9: Traction — cold email, LinkedIn, Loom videos, follow-up cadence; target 3–5 first clients on pilot pricing
   - Weeks 10–12: Scale — full-price contracts, sales frameworks, objection handling, repeatable acquisition loops

## Pricing Ladder

| Phase | Price | Duration |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Pilot | $2K–$5K/mo | Months 1–3 |
| Peer | $5K–$10K/mo | Months 4–9 |
| Real | $10K–$20K/mo | Month 10+ |

Five clients at $15K monthly approaches $1M ARR. Ten gets you well past it. The trap is staying at pilot pricing forever — margins collapse and operators hate the business by month nine.

## Brand Is What's Left

Anybody with a decent IQ can build a website calling themselves an AI GTM agency. The tooling moat is gone. What's left is brand: your numbers, your case studies, your clients. Give the information for free. Charge for implementation.
