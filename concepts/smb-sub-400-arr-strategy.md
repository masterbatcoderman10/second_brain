# SMB Sub-$400 ARR Strategy

The thesis that $1M ARR is achievable by targeting SMBs with a sub-$400/month productized service or SaaS, as articulated by Shubh Agrawal (@ShubhAgrawal26) in response to Craig Weiss's "1m arr is not as hard as it sounds."

## Core Math

| Variable | Value |
|---|---|
| Target ARR | $1,000,000 |
| Monthly Revenue Target | $83,333 |
| Price Point | $350/month |
| Customers Needed | ~237 |
| Deals/Month (Target) | 40 |
| Close Rate | 30% |
| Calls/Month Required | 120 |

## Key Principles

1. **Price low to retain long**: SMBs churn within 3–5 months at $2–5K/month; they retain 20–30 months at sub-$400
2. **80–90% gross margins required**: Service must be highly scalable with minimal variable cost
3. **Productized or outcome-based**: Monthly value delivered without input from the client
4. **No-brainer offer with guarantee**: Qualification gates + concrete promise
5. **Scale to 10–30M is easier once solved**: Low-ticket SMB model is more defensible than high-ticket agency

## The Agency Ceiling

High-ticket services ($3–5K/month) for SMBs → 3–4 month churn → CAC rises + margins shrink as you fight for replacements. Most agencies never cross $3M ARR; the $3M → $10M journey is described as "eating glass."

## Upmarket Path

After cracking the SMB model, move upmarket — but difficult for newcomers who lack credibility at enterprise levels.
