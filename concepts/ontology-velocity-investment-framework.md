# Ontology Velocity Investment Framework

## Overview

Antonio Linares's investment framework centered on **Ontology Velocity** — the rate at which a company's proprietary data and AI capabilities compound relative to competitors. The framework identifies how AI confusion creates asymmetric stock market opportunities.

## Core Concepts

### The Costco Algorithm

Nick Sleep's term for **Economies of Scale Shared**: companies obsessed with delivering more value to end customers per dollar spent. This compounds goodwill → more capital → lower prices → more customers → repeat. The flywheel.

AI removes the speed limit on the Costco Algorithm by enabling network effects and proprietary model training at scale.

### Ontology Velocity

The compounding rate of proprietary data advantage. The flywheel:

1. Hard thing attracts many users
2. Many users generate proprietary behavioral data
3. Proprietary data trains AI models competitors can't replicate
4. Better AI delivers more value per token
5. More value → more engagement → more data
6. Gap compounds until uncrossable

**A 0.1% faster Ontology Velocity than competitors = eventual winner takes most.**

### Singularity Scalers

Companies that have achieved Ontology Velocity. They accelerate free cash flow per share growth as AI capabilities compound, abstracting away value chain complexity to become exponentially more powerful at near-zero marginal cost.

### Singularity Asymmetries

The investment opportunity: the market will often unanimously declare AI has killed a company precisely when that company has achieved Ontology Velocity. Result: exponentially rising earning power + exponentially decaying stock price = the asymmetric opportunity gap.

## Framework: Identifying Singularity Scalers

**Rule of thumb checklist:**

- Does the company do something hard that attracts many users?
- Count the components: UX, regulatory, manufacturing, distribution, trust — more = deeper moat
- Has the company scaled far enough to accumulate more proprietary data than anyone else in its category?
- Is the company actively training AI on that data, or leaving it on the table?
- Does the resulting product experience pull ahead of generic models in ways users can feel?
- **Ultimate litmus test:** Does the company have a cult user base?

## The Company's DNA (3 Properties)

Companies that handle uncertainty well:
1. Obsessed with end customers
2. Iterate faster than competitors
3. Willing to disrupt themselves

These properties cause revenue to rise faster than costs over the long term — compounding goodwill eventually becomes a gravitational force.

## The Math of Market Confusion

If a stock drops 90%, you can buy 10x more shares per dollar. At $1M allocated, that's $9M in extra purchasing power handed by the market's confusion. At $10M, $90M. At $100M, $900M.

**The more confused the market, the bigger the gift.**
