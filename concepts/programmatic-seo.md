# Programmatic SEO

## Definition

Programmatic SEO is a content strategy that treats SEO pages as **building blocks** rather than one-off pieces. Instead of writing each page individually, you design a structure once, then replicate it across hundreds or thousands of keyword variations at scale — targeting demand patterns programmatically rather than manually.

**Core principle** (from [[denohawari]]): instead of thinking in terms of pages, think in terms of systems.

## How It Works

Traditional SEO: write a page → target 1-5 keywords → wait months → repeat

Programmatic SEO: write a structure once → replicate across all variations → each page targets a specific demand pattern → compound over time

## The 10 Patterns

From [[denohawari]]'s playbook — each pattern is a replicable page structure:

### 1. Best Pages (Curation)
**Pattern:** `best [category]`, `top [tools]`
**Examples:** best CRM for startups, top AI tools for marketers, best project management software
**Why it works:** captures high-intent users at the decision stage
**Pro tip:** don't list everything, make a clear #1 recommendation

### 2. Comparisons
**Pattern:** `[x] vs [y]`, `[x] alternatives`
**Examples:** webflow vs wordpress, notion vs coda, figma alternatives
**Why it works:** targets users actively deciding between options
**Pro tip:** include "who this is for" — AI overview snippets pull this

### 3. Use-Case Pages (Personas)
**Pattern:** `[product] for [audience]`
**Examples:** CRM for real estate, payroll software for agencies, task management for freelancers
**Why it works:** higher relevance = higher conversion
**Pro tip:** reuse the same structure, swap only the persona

### 4. Integrations
**Pattern:** `[tool] + [tool]`
**Examples:** hubspot + salesforce, slack + asana, zapier + airtable
**Why it works:** captures users already using another product
**Pro tip:** easiest way to steal competitor traffic

### 5. Templates / Generators
**Pattern:** `[type] template`, `[x] generator`
**Examples:** invoice template, cold email generator, resume builder
**Why it works:** instant value → high engagement + sharing
**Pro tip:** make it usable immediately, no signup friction

### 6. Converters
**Pattern:** `[x] to [y]`
**Examples:** pdf to word, USD to EUR, kg to lbs
**Why it works:** repeat usage + massive search volume
**Pro tip:** add related conversions to multiply traffic

### 7. Examples / Inspiration
**Pattern:** `[type] examples`
**Examples:** SaaS landing page examples, cold email examples, portfolio website examples
**Why it works:** highly shareable + strong discovery traffic
**Pro tip:** add filters/categories to increase time on page

### 8. Directories
**Pattern:** `[category] tools`
**Examples:** AI copywriting tools, email marketing software, CRM platforms
**Why it works:** captures broad research traffic at scale
**Pro tip:** keep updating, freshness = rankings

### 9. Glossary
**Pattern:** `what is [term]`
**Examples:** what is LLM SEO, what is API, what does CRM mean
**Why it works:** builds authority + internal linking
**Pro tip:** link these into higher-intent pages

### 10. Localization
**Pattern:** translate everything
**Examples:** qué es SEO, was ist CRM, logiciel CRM gratuit
**Why it works:** new markets + lower competition
**Pro tip:** don't just translate, localize intent for that country/city

## Scaling Math

- 1 structure × 10 variations × 10 keywords = 100+ pages
- 100 pages × 10 keyword targets = 1,000+ keywords
- This is how smaller teams outperform companies with 10x the budget
