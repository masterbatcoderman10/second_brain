# Upwork Cover Letter Template

Archie H.'s cover letter structure that earned over $1M on Upwork. Designed to overcome the three failure modes of the 86% who never earn on Upwork: self-focus over client-focus, quantity over quality, and rapid-fire generic proposals.

## The Three 86%er Failure Modes

1. **Self-focus** — Proposals that start with "I" instead of the client's needs. Test: if the first word is "I", rewrite.
2. **Quantity over quality** — Copy-pasting the same proposal with <10 word changes. Playing a dangerous game.
3. **Rapid-fire generic proposals** — Spending <1 minute reading the job description before applying.

## The Cover Letter Structure

### 1. Hook (2–3 sentences)
Personalized opening referencing a specific client pain point or project. Not about you — about their problem.

> "Hey [Client Name], sounds like there could be a fit here. I've done a ton of [their niche] work. I know it can be really challenging and time-consuming to [specific problem they face]."

### 2. Empathy (1–2 sentences)
Demonstrate you understand the client's frustrations in detail.

> "I know it can be really challenging and time-consuming to plan out the video, source B-roll, cut and regrade the footage, handle sound design, and produce an excellent end product. In fact, most of my clients tell me that they don't have time to spend a thousand hours learning how to edit."

### 3. Simple Solution (1 sentence)
Client-centric close to the empathy section.

> "The simple solution: let me handle it."

### 4. Proof / Results (bullet points)
Quantified results for past clients. Format: *Client name, specific metric, outcome.*

> "Insane Products — 3 viral videos with over 2 million total views. Core Tech — 87% average video retention with 250,000 views. Bonanza Books — corporate explainer video that led to a 200% increase in leads within three months."

### 5. CTA (1 sentence)
Crystal-clear next step.

> "Sound like a fit? Shoot me a message and let's organize a time to meet today. I'm looking forward [Client Name]."

### 6. P.S. (1 sentence)
Social proof amplification and secondary action.

> "P.S. I also attached two working samples that I think you might like, but you can check out more of my work on my Upwork profile."

## Core Principle: Experiment

The template is a structure to customize, not copy-paste. The key to going from the 14% (earn something) to the 0.14% (top earners) is experimentation — test different cover letters, measure results, iterate.
