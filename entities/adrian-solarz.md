## Core Framework: The Bias Stack

The thesis is that viral UGC is not "good content" in the abstract — it is content that **systematically activates specific cognitive biases at each stage** of the viewer journey.

**The bias stack** (applied in order across a campaign):

| Stage | Biases Activated |
|-------|-----------------|
| First frame | Novelty bias, Orienting Response, Contrast Effect |
| Hook | Information Gap Theory, FOMO |
| Pain section | Empathy Gap, Loss Aversion, In-Group Bias |
| Mechanism bridge | Zeigarnik Effect, Anchoring Effect |
| Transformation | Narrative Transportation, Awe Response |
| CTA | Reciprocity, Commitment & Consistency |
| Comments | Social Proof Cascade |

## Notable Bias Principles

- **Novelty Bias:** Audit top 30 reels in niche — use visual elements appearing in fewer than 3 of 30
- **Orienting Response:** Add 1 controlled imperfection in first 3 seconds (micro-movement, ambient sound)
- **Information Gap:** Counterintuitive, specific claim that creates cognitive tension
- **Empathy Gap:** Present-tense pain language puts viewer inside the emotion, not observing it
- **Loss Aversion:** Frame cost of inaction in concrete, ongoing terms
- **Anchoring:** Mention failed alternative costs before stating product price
- **Zeigarnik Effect:** Engineer 2–3 open loops per script to carry viewer through transitions
- **Narrative Transportation:** Use exact persona language from customer reviews — specificity drives depth of engagement
