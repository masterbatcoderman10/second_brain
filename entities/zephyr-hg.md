## Source

[The $12K/Month Service AI Can't Replace (And How to Build It By August)](https://x.com/Zephyr_hg/status/2045575416010686889) — A guide to building an AI Implementation Consulting service targeting 10-50 person SMBs. The core claim: as AI gets better, the human judgment layer — understanding a specific business's operations and designing which workflows to automate — becomes more valuable, not less.

## Core Thesis

**AI Implementation Consulting** or **Fractional Ops**: someone who audits how a business actually runs, identifies the 3-5 workflows bleeding hours, designs and builds AI/automation systems to replace them, then trains the team to run them.

**Why AI can't replace it**: AI builds workflows; it doesn't pick which workflows to build. Picking the right ones requires human judgment, accountability, and context about the specific business.

## The $12K/Month Math

- Typical SMB: 20-person team losing 15 hours/week to manual work
- AI implementation returns 60 hours/month
- At $75/hour blended rate = $4,500/month in recovered labor
- $12,000/month retainer is "trivial math" against $18-25K recovered

## Three Offer Structure

1. **Audit only** — $3,000 flat, 1 week, 30-page playbook; 20% convert to full engagement
2. **Build only** — $15,000 fixed, 6 weeks, 3-5 working systems
3. **Full engagement** — $12,000/month, 6-month minimum: audit + build + train + ongoing tuning

## Tool Stack

n8n + Make for automation. Context systems (so AI knows each client's business without re-explaining). Prompt engineering for fast context building.
