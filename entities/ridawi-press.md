    10|## 1. Overview
    11|
    12|**Ridawi Press** is a free online publishing press producing English translations of books by Sunni scholars, along with whitepapers, refutations of heterodox sects, and visual infographics.
    13|
    14|**All content is free.** The press grants blanket permission to download, share, print, and even sell their materials for profit.
    15|
    16|**Central Figure:** Imam Ahmad Rida Khan al-Baraylawi (known as "Alahazrat") — the vast majority of published works are either his own writings, translations of his Urdu works, or scholarly works defending his positions.
    17|
    18|**Primary Translator / Editor:** Abu Hasan — student of Islamic law. All book description pages credit him as author/compiler. Contact: abu.hasan@ridawipress.org

   206|

   208|
   209|- Full name: Imam Ahmad Rida Khan al-Baraylawi
   210|- Born: Shawwal, 1272 AH
   211|- Ancestors: Originally from Qandahar, Afghanistan; family migrated to India
   212|- Position: Held a distinguished scholarly and spiritual position under Mughal patronage
   213|- Father: Mawlana Karim Ali Khan
   214|- Grandfather: Mawlana Naqi Ali Khan
   215|- Scholarly output: Massive — the Fatawa Ridawiyyah alone is a 30-volume collection of fatwas
   216|- Renowned for: Mastery of hadith sciences, defense of orthodox Sunni creed, prolific writing
   217|- Term "Maulana": The book discusses the significance and proper usage of this title
   218|- The press publishes both his original works AND works defending his positions against critics (Deobandis, Nuh Keller, Minhaju'l Quran)

   241|## 9. Theological Position
   242|
   243|Ridawi Press positions itself within the **Ahl al-Sunnah wa'l Jama'ah** (Sunni orthodox) tradition, specifically aligned with the **Barelvi** school. The press:
   244|
   245|- Publishes translations of classical Sunni scholarship (Hanafi law, Ash'ari theology)
   246|- Produces **refutations** of: Deobandis (takfir disputes), Nuh Keller (on takfir authority), Minhaju'l Quran (Islamic scholar critiques)
   247|- Focuses heavily on hadith sciences and proper creed (aqidah)
   248|- Publishes works on Mawlid (birth celebrations of the Prophet) and Sufi-related subjects
