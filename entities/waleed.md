Cousin. Runs an interior decoration and finishing business in Bangalore under the name **UberHaus** — designs and develops interior finishes for residential and commercial spaces. Currently 0 digital presence; clients acquired purely via word of mouth.

---

## Strategic Role

- Family trust baseline — lower friction for first revenue loop
- Existing network in Bangalore's interior design and construction ecosystem
- Business-minded — can identify pain points, close deals, and report back real market feedback
- His customer base is a live distribution channel for AI-adjacent products and services

## Active Ideas

### 1. UberHaus Landing Page + SEO (low cost, high leverage)

- 0 digital presence today. A clean landing page with local SEO could capture existing demand immediately
- If it works in Bangalore interior design → replicable to other niches (the real upside)
- Revenue model: leads, not product
- Vectors: AI-generated content, AI-generated ads
- Risk: SEO timelines are long; leads may not come fast enough

### 2. AI-Generated UGC Ads for UberHaus

- Before/after reels, design showcases, local targeting
- Bangalore educated working class = heavy scrollers, Instagram/Reels audience fit
- Interior design isn't classic UGC but can be adapted (client testimonials, process timelapse, material reveals)
- Revenue model: client acquisition for UberHaus (not standalone income)
- Vectors: AI-generated ads, AI-generated content
- Risk: Content is cheap to produce; distribution and creative quality matter more

### 3. Internal Quotation + Material DB Tool (zero-cost to Waleed, resell-able)

- Everything manual: quotations typed from scratch, no tracking of past materials/prices across projects
- Build a simple DB: project history, material list, unit prices, auto-populate next quote
- Free for Waleed. He resells/installs for peers in the Bangalore interior market
- Revenue model: resell to other businesses (one-to-many)
|- Vectors: AI agent automation, mobile apps
- Risk: Very low. Needs validation that other businesses in his network will actually pay

### 4. SaaS Project Management Tool (WhatsApp replacement for job sites)

- Current state: WhatsApp chaos — photos, audio, progress notes scattered across chat threads
- Two versions: manager view (full oversight) + worker view (task-focused)
- Core features: photo threads with audio messages, per-room/named-section tracking, document storage (plan PDFs, material specs), supply item lists, progress flow
- Market signal: one competitor exists in Bangalore market but pricing is hidden behind a sales call and likely expensive; gap at low price point
- Revenue model: SaaS subscription, inexpensive price point
|- Vectors: SaaS, mobile apps
- Status: **Market validation needed** before building

## Sequencing Logic

Anti-dispersion applies. Only ONE vector in active execution at a time.

Priority order (subject to validation):
1. **#3 first** — free for Waleed, lowest risk, immediate utility, real usage data before we market to his network

3. **#4 requires market validation** — search for existing tools, confirm competitor pricing gap
4. **#1** — landing page is a long-tail play; do last or alongside #2
