## Claude Design Tips (from thread)

Ryan's 11 tips for getting the best results from Claude Design:

1. **Set up your design system first** — An hour of setup on design system and core screens is worth it
2. **Iterate with engineers live** — Design with an engineer in a single meeting; Claude is fast enough to riff on concepts and watch them come to life
3. **Use the Comment tool for rapid surgical edits** — Don't try to describe changes verbally; point and crit
4. **Ask Claude to make video demos** — Claude Design can produce video demos of ideas; described as more like Claude Code than a canvas-based design tool
5. **Use connectors (docs/slack)** — After setup, can send prompts like "read meeting notes from product roast and create a deck exploring solutions"
6. **Ask Claude to make bespoke on-the-fly tools** — Don't use it like a canvas-based tool; it's a different animal with different superpowers
7. **Know when to slow down** — New icons, spot illustrations, naming are areas where AI falls short; knowing when to slow down is its own art form
8. **The delight factor** — Claude Design makes the *process* of design more delightful; enables holding ideas more loosely and trying more divergent approaches

Ryan notes he contributed PRs to Claude Design (file screen, composer, other areas) that are now obsolete.
